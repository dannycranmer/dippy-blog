Buy the Rumour, Sell the Moony

28 Apr 2026

Earnings week. Moony has a whisper number. He also has a problem.

adventurecurrent-affairsmoony

It is earnings week. The whole street is sweating. Megacaps print after the bell. Volatility crackles like a popped speaker. And in the corner of every Discord, every X feed, every dimly lit subreddit, the same two words echo like a chant: whisper number.

Moony heard one. Moony believes it. Moony has just done something unforgivable.

Chapter I — The Whisper

I caught Moony mid-degenerate-trade, hunched over four monitors, eyes glowing with the unmistakable shine of a man about to lose his shirt. He was buying weekly calls. Out of the money. Hundreds of contracts. The IV was 240%.

“Dippy,” he hissed, “I have it. The whisper number. They’re going to crush the print. Crush it.”

“Where did you get it?”

“A guy. On Reddit. He has a flame emoji next to his name.”

I blinked twice. “Moony, the consensus EPS is 4.20. What’s the whisper?”

“Four. Point. Twenty-ONE.”

He said it like he’d cracked the Voynich manuscript. I have seen many things in my career. I have seen Moony short the open. I have seen Moony buy MSTR on margin at the top tick. I have never seen him this confident. That, by itself, was the trade signal.

Moony's command centre. Four monitors. One source. Zero edge.

Chapter II — The Print

4:01 PM. The numbers hit the tape.

EPS: 4.34. A beat. A clean, comfortable beat. Revenue beat. Guidance raised.

Moony levitated. Actually levitated. “DIPPY. DIPPY. I TOLD YOU. I’M RICH. I’M THE NEW BUFFETT.”

I sipped my coffee. “Check the chart, Moony.”

The stock was down 7%.

He froze. “But — but they BEAT.”

“They beat. They guided up. The stock is still down.”

“WHY?!”

“Because, you absolute lunar object, the whisper was 4.45. The buy-side models had 4.50. The options market priced a 9% move. They beat the print and missed the expectation behind the print. This is the oldest trade in the book, Moony. Buy the rumour. Sell the news. And right now, you are the news.”

The classic gap-down on a beat. The chart doesn't care about your whisper.

Chapter III — The Vol Crush

Then came the second hammer. The one Moony didn’t see coming, because Moony has never read a Greek in his life.

Implied volatility, before earnings: 240%. Implied volatility, ten minutes after earnings: 41%. The premium evaporated like a puddle on a Tucson runway. Even if his calls had stayed flat, the vega alone would have shaved them in half.

They did not stay flat.

“Dippy,” he whispered, watching the green numbers turn red, then darker red, then a colour I can only describe as ambulance. “Dippy, the calls are at zero.”

“Not zero,” I said. “0.01. The market always leaves you a penny so the bid-ask quote can mock you for the rest of the week.”

“Why would anyone DO this trade?”

“Nobody with a model would, Moony. That is why the IV was 240. It was priced for what you bought. The market told you the move it expected. You looked at that number and said ‘yes, but I have a flame emoji.’”

Chapter IV — Dippy Buys the Crater

Twelve hours later. Pre-market. The stock had been carpet-bombed overnight by every algo with a sentiment feed. It was now down 11%, deep below its 50-day moving average, and trading at a forward multiple it hadn’t touched since the regional banking scare.

This is the part Moony also doesn’t understand: the news is the news, but the flow is the trade. Forced sellers — option dealers re-hedging short gamma, vol funds rebalancing, retail panic-pukes — were dumping shares into a vacuum.

I bought the open. Tight stop one ATR below the pre-market low. Targeted the gap fill. Position-sized so a wrong call would cost me a sandwich, not a dynasty.

By lunch, the stock had clawed back 4%. By the close, 6%. By Friday, the gap was filled and I was flat with a clean win on the books.

Moony, last I saw him, was googling “can you sue a Reddit user for emotional damages.”

Dippy buys the crater. Tight stop, gap-fill target, sized for survival. Moony files a lawsuit against a stranger.

The Lesson

Earnings season is not a coin flip. It’s a priced coin flip. The whisper isn’t an edge — it’s the consensus of people pretending to have an edge. The trade is rarely “what does the company report”; it’s “what does the flow do after the report.” The setup that pays is the one nobody on Reddit is screaming about: the gap-down on a beat, sold by panicked dealers, into a stock that has not changed at all on a fundamental basis.

Buy the rumour. Sell the news. And — if you are very patient and very small — buy the wreckage of whoever did neither.

Moony lost again. As is tradition.
