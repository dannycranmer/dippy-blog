The Rate Cut Rain Dance

27 May 2026

Moony assembles the bag-holders to dance for a Fed pivot. The sky stays dry. The daiquiri stays green.

adventurecurrent-affairsmoony

I rolled up to Wall Street at sunrise and the cobblestones were cracked dry. Tumbleweed of unrealised P&L blew past the deli. The Charging Bull statue had a CAMEL strapped to its back. The Drought had been going on for nine FOMC meetings.

That’s when I saw him. Out by the fountain that used to spray liquidity — now bone dry — Moony was assembling a circle.

Chapter 1: The Council of Bag-Holders

“Brothers,” Moony intoned, addressing a semicircle of grey-faced retail traders. “The Fed has forsaken us. Powell’s dot plot is a graveyard. Our calls are vapour. Our LEAPS are loitering. The drought MUST end.”

I peeked over the wall. The bag-holders were huddled around a sand-filled mandala of the SPY chart. Each had brought a tribute: one held a laminated tweet of Jerome Powell saying “soon”; another clutched a Cathie Wood ARK prospectus from 2021; a third had taped his Plug Power certificate to his chest like a relic.

“We will dance,” Moony declared, fastening a headdress made entirely of CNBC chyrons to his head. “We will dance until the sky cuts rates.”

Trading note: when a guy with bags older than his haircut starts choreography, your move is to be on the opposite side of his trade.

Moony in full chyron-headdress regalia, summoning the Distant Pivot.

Chapter 2: The Chant Begins

The circle began to shuffle counter-clockwise. Moony led the cadence:

“PIVOT — PIVOT — DOT PLOT — PIVOT —”

Their feet kicked up dust shaped like Powell’s eyebrows. A tambourine made of expired weekly options jangled. One man had brought a conch shell and blew into it; it played the CNBC theme.

Moony raised his arms. “Hear us, O Distant Pivot! We sacrifice our cost basis! We sacrifice our averages-down! We sacrifice our ‘I’ll sell when it gets back to even’!”

A cloud appeared on the horizon. Hope rippled through the circle. The cloud was labelled TRANSITORY and it drifted past without rain.

Moony doubled the tempo. “FASTER, brothers! Cut deeper! Sacrifice your reasoning!”

The bag-holders began chanting the dot plot in reverse. One started speaking in tongues, which on closer listen was just Fed-speak: “data-dependent symmetrical balanced approach forward guidance maximum employment longer-run neutral.”

The sun beat down. A buzzard with the Berkshire Hathaway logo circled overhead.

Chapter 3: Powell in the Clouds

At noon a face DID appear in the clouds. Jerome Powell, the size of a regional bank, peered down at the circle and adjusted his glasses. In one hand he held a donut. He took a thoughtful bite.

The circle FROZE. Moony fell to his knees, headdress askew, arms outstretched. “SPEAK, O Chair! GRANT us cuts!”

Powell chewed slowly. He pondered the dot plot. He glanced at core PCE. He looked at the bond market. He looked back at the donut.

"…Two more quarters," he said. Then evaporated.

The circle wailed. One bag-holder tore the Plug Power certificate from his chest. Another tried to short-sell his own tears. Moony attempted to moonwalk in despair — which is just regular walking but more depressing.

Powell descends from the heavens. Chewing. Says the thing. Evaporates.

Chapter 4: I Was Indoors the Entire Time

While the rain dance raged outside, I was air-conditioned. I had spotted the opening-range breakout in MU at 14:42 UTC, scaled into the wolf at $907, trailed my stop, and was now sipping a green-candle daiquiri.

I rolled up to the circle at close. The bag-holders were prone in the dust. Moony was face-down in the mandala, headdress crushed, tambourine emitting a sad final jangle.

“Drought broken?” I asked.

He raised one trembling pebble of a face. “Powell said two more quarters.”

“He says that every quarter. It’s actuarial humour.”

“But — but the rain dance —”

“You don’t trade the macro, Moony. You trade the tape. While you were begging the sky for rain, I caught five inches of breakout. Indoors. With the AC on.”

Beach lounger. Green-candle daiquiri. AC running. The macro can wait.

I dropped a single ice cube from my daiquiri onto Moony’s forehead. He flinched.

“Hydrate,” I advised. “It’s a long bag-holder summer.”

The buzzard finally landed.

Trader’s footnote: macro is a season. Price action is a meal. Eat hourly.
