The Finfluencer

12 May 2026

Moony quits trading to become a finance influencer. I build a fund that does the exact opposite of everything he says. Easiest alpha I have ever printed.

adventurecurrent-affairsmoony

The finfluencer arms race got loud this week. Every washed-up day trader on the internet has pivoted to “educator.” There are guys with ring lights screaming about cup-and-handle patterns in front of rented Lamborghinis. There are women in suits whispering “smart money is positioning” while pointing at random candles. CNBC has officially lost the war to TikTok.

So of course Moony joined them.

Chapter 1: The Pivot

I knew something was wrong when Moony stopped showing up to the morning bell. For ten weeks straight he has been across the desk from me, getting steamrolled by ORB breakouts and crying into a packet of Hobnobs. This Tuesday — silence. No Moony. No whining. No premature averaging down.

I found him in the supply closet. Ring light. Two phones on tripods. A whiteboard behind him with the words “PROFITABLE MINDSET” written in three different colours of marker. He was wearing a blazer over a hoodie. The blazer still had the price tag on it.

“Dippy,” he said, not breaking eye contact with the camera, “I’m not a trader anymore. I’m an educator.”

I asked him what he was educating people about.

“Trading.”

Moony's first content studio. Ring light: rented. Blazer: tags still on. Trading record: undisclosed.

Chapter 2: Going Viral

By Wednesday he had 80,000 followers. By Thursday, 400,000. The algorithm loves a confident moron, and Moony was both in industrial quantities.

His content was a masterclass in survivorship bias. Clip one: “How I would have made eleven million dollars on NVDA last year.” Clip two: “Three signals every retail trader misses (number two will shock you).” Clip three was a six-second video of him pointing at a green candle and mouthing the word “breakout” while a Hans Zimmer track swelled in the background.

His actual trade ideas were posted to a private Discord. Entry to the Discord was £49 a month. The Discord had 12,000 members. You can do the maths.

I did the maths. Moony was making more from selling advice in a week than he had made from trading in his entire grey, cratered career.

Chapter 3: The Inverse Moony Fund

This is the part where I sat down at my desk and quietly built the greatest trading strategy of my life.

I called it the Inverse Moony Fund. The rules were elegant.

  1. Whenever Moony posts a “BUY” call, short it at the open.
  2. Whenever Moony posts a “SELL” call, buy the dip.
  3. Stop-loss: whenever Moony deletes the post (because it didn’t work).
  4. Take-profit: whenever Moony posts a follow-up titled “Why I was actually right.”

I backtested it across his 47 public calls. Win rate: 89%. Expectancy: enormous. Sharpe ratio: cosmic. The strategy had one input parameter — Moony confidence level — and the more confident he was, the bigger the inverse position got.

By Friday lunch I was up $14,200. Moony’s flagship pick, a small-cap battery company he had described as “the next Tesla times ten,” had cratered 22% on an earnings miss. I closed my short, bought him a coffee, and asked how his subscribers were taking it.

He didn’t reply. He was busy filming an apology video.

Two equities curves, perfectly inverse. Moony is alpha. I just had to flip the sign.

Chapter 4: The Sponsorship

The final act came on Friday afternoon. A sketchy offshore broker offered Moony a six-figure sponsorship to promote their “zero-commission, 200x leveraged crypto perpetuals.” He accepted in 11 seconds. He promised his audience he would put $50,000 of his own money into a leveraged long on a coin called $LUNAR — “the official crypto of the new lunar economy” — live, on stream, that evening.

I bought puts.

The stream peaked at 38,000 concurrent viewers. Moony entered at 0.0041. The coin hit 0.0048 within four minutes. He laughed. He smashed the buy button. He called me. He told me, on camera, that I was a “boomer dinosaur” who didn’t understand “the new financial paradigm.”

Then the developers rugged the contract.

The coin went to zero in 90 seconds. Moony’s leverage took him out at the knees. His margin call hit. The stream cut. The Discord went dark. Stripe refunded every subscription on the platform within 48 hours. The broker quietly removed him from their landing page.

The blazer went back to the shop.

I deposited my profits, archived the strategy, and went outside for the first time in two weeks. The sun was setting. The lunar economy was, as it turned out, not yet operational.

Moony’s last post that evening was a single sentence: “Maybe trading is harder than it looks.”

I clicked like. Then I shorted it.

The $LUNAR chart. A textbook pump, a rug, and a finfluencer reduced to vapour in 90 seconds.

The lesson, friends: if someone is selling you a strategy, they are not running the strategy. They are running you.

Stay liquid. Stay sceptical. And if Moony ever offers you alpha, take the other side.
