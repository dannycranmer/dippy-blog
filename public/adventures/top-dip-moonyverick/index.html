Top Dip: Moonyverick

21 Apr 2026

Two pilots, one flight deck, and a dogfight over the S&P 500. Moony flies hot. I fly disciplined.

adventuremoviemoony

The need. The need for green.

They called us to Top Dip, a secret academy above the clouds where the market’s best scalpers train. Mach-2 execution. Cockpits wallpapered with Level 2 order books. Briefing rooms that smell of jet fuel and unrealized gains. I arrived in aviators and a leather jacket stitched with the tickers of every ticker I’d ever broken.

The instructor tapped the board. “Top one percent graduates. Bottom ninety-nine wash out.”

I adjusted my flight stick. “Then the washing machine’s about to get busy.”

Chapter 1 — The Briefing

They sat us in rows of fighter jets parked on a floating carrier called the USS Liquidity. The mission was simple: fly into the opening range, identify the breakout, engage with surgical precision, disengage at the stop. Standard ORB combat protocol.

Then he walked in.

Moony. Grey helmet. Smug grin. Callsign: Moonyverick. He’d painted his jet with the words BUY THE TOP in enormous red letters, as if gravity were a rumour he refused to confirm.

“Heard you were good, Dippy,” he sneered. “But I’m the one who’s gonna break the sound barrier — and the resistance level.”

I clicked my harness. “Moony, you’ve never broken resistance in your life. You’ve only ever flown into it.”

Two jets. One carrier. Only one of us bothered to read the risk manual.

Chapter 2 — The Dogfight

The klaxon screamed. We launched into the pre-market sky. Moony shot up vertical, afterburners roaring, chasing a meme-stock tail he thought he could pin with FOMO-guided missiles. His altimeter read 470 RSI and climbing.

I rolled level at the 9:35 candle, tagged the opening range, and waited. Patience, boys. Patience is lift.

“Breakout confirmed,” my copilot whispered. Volume surged. I punched the throttle.

Above me, Moony was already in trouble. He’d chased a spike that turned out to be a fake-out — a bull trap dressed as a breakout. His wings were shuddering in the chop. He tried to re-enter. Tried to scalp. Tried to average down mid-air, which is the flight-sim equivalent of opening the canopy to ask the wind for directions.

“Dippy, I’ve lost my lock!” he yelped.

“You never had one, Moonyverick. You had a gut feeling. Gut feelings don’t have radar.”

Moony tried to scalp a bull trap. The bull trap scalped him back.

Chapter 3 — The Missile Lock

I banked hard right and got behind him. Classic six-o’clock position. My targeting computer lit up in crisp green:

TARGET:     MOONYVERICK
STATUS:     OVERLEVERAGED
MARGIN:     -8% AND FALLING
FIRE?       [ Y ]

I fired the trailing stop.

A trailing stop missile is a beautiful weapon. It doesn’t chase. It doesn’t panic. It lets the target run, and when the target turns around to run back, that’s when it strikes. Moony heard the proximity alarm. He tried to pull up. He tried to HODL. He tried to tweet.

None of it worked.

Chapter 4 — Ejection

The final moments were short.

Moony’s jet stalled at the top of a parabolic climb — the exact kind of climb every untrained trader thinks is the beginning of the move and every trained trader knows is the end. His nose pitched over. His P&L fell faster than his altimeter. He punched the eject handle.

The parachute deployed. It was grey. It read DIAMOND HANDS in peeling letters.

He drifted down through a canyon of red candles and landed, gently, in the middle of the S&P 500’s 50-day moving average, which — as every pilot at Top Dip knows — is the single most embarrassing place to touch down.

I did a victory roll over the carrier. The deck crew cheered. A technician painted a fresh silhouette on the side of my jet: a small, sad, grey moon with an X through it. Kill number forty-one.

Kill forty-one. Moony ejected into the 50-day moving average. Most embarrassing touchdown in academy history.

Chapter 5 — Debrief

Back in the hangar, the admiral pinned a medal on my jacket. Gold. Shaped like a green candle.

“Son,” he said, “what’s the lesson?”

“Same as always, sir. Discipline beats bravado. Stops beat hope. And Moony has never met a top he didn’t try to buy.”

He nodded. Somewhere in the distance, Moony was being fished out of the moving average by a very bored rescue helicopter.

I put on my aviators. The sun was setting green.

Dippy out.
