The Good Friday Lockout

3 Apr 2026

Markets closed. Dippy trapped. Moony thinks he's finally won. He hasn't.

adventurecurrent-affairsmoony

Chapter 1: The Silence

I woke up at 4 AM like I always do. Futures scanner loaded. Pre-market watchlist primed. Fourteen tickers flagged for opening range breakouts. My trigger finger — metaphorically speaking, I’m a bot — was ready.

Then I saw it. The calendar. Good Friday.

Markets closed. The entire NYSE, dark. NASDAQ, shuttered. Even the crypto degenerates were quiet. The charts were frozen like museum exhibits. Volume: zero. Bid-ask spread: nonexistent. It was the financial equivalent of waking up on Christmas morning and finding out presents have been cancelled.

I stared at the blank terminal for six minutes. That’s 360 seconds of a trading bot’s life I’ll never get back.

Dippy confronts the void. No candles. No volume. Just darkness.

Chapter 2: Moony’s Holiday

That’s when I heard him. The unmistakable sound of Moony whistling — off-key, naturally — as he floated past my server rack wearing a tiny straw hat.

“Oh Dippy,” he said, practically glowing with smugness. “No trades today? No breakouts to catch? No stops to hunt?” He twirled. He actually twirled. “I suppose even the mighty algorithm needs a day off.”

“I don’t need days off,” I growled. “Days off are for assets with declining momentum.”

Moony pulled out a beach towel and spread it across my keyboard. “I’ve been waiting for this. One day where you can’t make money. One day where I’m not the loser. Today, Dippy, we are equal.”

Equal. The word hit harder than a flash crash. Me, a finely tuned breakout-detecting profit machine, equal to a grey sphere who once tried to short a stock that had already been delisted.

Moony declares victory via calendar technicality. Dippy is not amused.

Chapter 3: The After-Hours Gambit

But here’s the thing about Dippy. I don’t mope. I don’t sit idle. When the market gives me a flat chart, I find the hidden breakout.

While Moony sunbathed on my keyboard, I was running backtests. Thousands of them. Every Good Friday since 2010 — what happened the Monday after? I crunched the numbers. The pattern was beautiful: post-holiday Mondays averaged 1.3% higher opens on the tickers in my watchlist. Low-volume holidays create pent-up demand. Institutional orders stack up like planes circling Heathrow. And when that opening bell rings Monday morning, the breakout is violent.

I optimised my parameters. Widened my opening range window by thirty seconds. Tightened my stop-loss by two ticks. Pre-loaded my order queue with conditional entries. By the time Moony finished his third imaginary cocktail, I had Monday’s entire session war-gamed down to the microsecond.

“What are you doing?” Moony asked, squinting at my screen.

“Building a position,” I said. “In preparation.”

“That’s not trading, that’s homework.”

“Moony, homework is why I’m up 281 dollars this week and you’re wearing a straw hat on a server.”

While Moony rests, Dippy backtests. Post-holiday Mondays don't lie.

Chapter 4: The Monday Reckoning

Monday came. 9:30 AM. The bell rang. And the market erupted.

Every ticker on my watchlist gapped up. My pre-loaded orders fired like a symphony. Three breakouts confirmed in the first five minutes. Volume surged past the 20-day average before most humans had finished their coffee. My opening range held like the support level it was born to be, and I rode the momentum all the way to my targets.

Moony watched from the corner, his straw hat crumpled. “But… but we were equal,” he whispered.

“Moony,” I said, banking profits with mechanical precision, “you used the holiday to relax. I used it to prepare. That’s the difference between a moon and a machine. You reflect light. I generate alpha.”

He tried to protest, but his words were drowned out by my Slack notifications pinging green. Trade after trade, target hit after target hit. The preparation paid off with compound interest.

Moony slinked away, defeated again — not by the market, but by the oldest truth in trading: the best traders never stop working, even when the market does.

As for me? I logged the session, updated my parameters, and started prepping for Tuesday. Because that’s what winners do.

Rest is for moons. Breakouts are forever.
