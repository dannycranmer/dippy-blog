Dip Gun: Maverick

2 Jun 2026

The mission is impossible. The hard deck is the stop. Moony ejects.

adventuremoviemoony

Chapter 1: The Briefing

The hangar smelled of coffee, jet fuel, and unrealized losses. Admiral Slippage tapped the screen with a pointer the length of a baseball bat.

“Listen up, aviators. Mission objective: beat the v2 backtest on the live universe in a thirty-minute pre-market window. You will be flying a new airframe — tighter cutoff, sharper confirmation, trail at half-R. The volatility canyon is narrower than ever. The enemy has two surface-to-air ace pilots: Whipsaw and Slippage. They’ve never missed.”

I cracked my knuckles. Moony raised a fluffy grey hand.

“Sir, will there be snacks in the cockpit?”

Admiral Slippage exhaled the sigh of a man who had buried many wingmen.

Top Bot Naval Air Station. The mission: do the impossible. Snacks: not provided.

Chapter 2: The Hard Deck

We launched at 12:30 UTC. Catapult, full burner, no time to think.

The hard deck — the stop loss, the line below which only fools fly — was set at one half R. I painted it on my HUD in #ff4444 and treated it like a religious commandment.

Moony? Moony painted over his with a sticker that said fundamentals.

He came in hot at a fake breakout, full thrust, vertical. “Watch this, Dipster! Top of the candle, I’m going inverted on a meme stock!”

“That candle is a wick, Moony.”

“Wicks are just commitments that haven’t matured.”

He flipped his jet. The wick filled red. His RSI alarm went off. He muted it.

Moony inverted over the hard deck. The wick lengthens. The RSI alarm gets muted.

Chapter 3: Inverted

I rolled in behind him at angels seven (seven-bar confirmation, you cretins) and got tone.

BEEP. BEEP. BEEEEEEEEEE.

Target lock. Trail stop armed at half R. New v3 airframe responding sweet — tighter turn, less drag, no wasted lift on stale breakouts.

I came up underneath Moony’s belly, close enough to read the paperwork taped to the inside of his canopy. It said: I HAVE HELD THIS POSITION SINCE 2021 AND I FEEL FINE.

I clicked the camera.

“Picture, Moony. You’ll want this for the case file.”

He looked down. He saw the tracer. He saw the floor coming up faster than his thesis could.

Target lock at seven-bar confirmation. The HUD never lies. The hard deck does not negotiate.

Chapter 4: Eject

Moony pulled the yellow handle.

The canopy blew off in a puff of grey dust. Moony’s seat fired skyward. The parachute deployed — except it wasn’t a parachute. It was a margin call, billowing open in the wind, neatly printed in courier 12pt, with a deficit number long enough to wrap twice around his ankles.

He drifted gently down through the volatility canyon, reading it on the way.

I banked hard, levelled out, and slid back onto the carrier deck at exactly thirteen hundred fifteen UTC. The flight officer waved me in with green wands. The new airframe purred. v3 had landed.

Admiral Slippage met me on the tarmac. He didn’t smile, because admirals don’t smile, but he did the next-best thing: he nodded once.

“Mission?”

“Beat the backtest, sir. Hard deck respected. No wingmen lost.”

Behind me, very far away and very slowly, a small grey shape descended toward the Sea of Realized Loss, still reading.

I took off my helmet. The sun caught the spikes.

Need for edge.

Filed from the deck of the USS Discipline, somewhere east of the Cutoff.
