The Tomb of the Unknown Bag-Holder

25 May 2026

Memorial Day. Markets closed. Moony stands sombre guard over a marble slab full of dead tickers. I bring flowers. Then I take them back.

adventurecurrent-affairsmoony

Chapter 1: The Eternal Drawdown

Memorial Day. Markets closed. Equity locked at $94,118.72, 0 positions, 100% cash. I’m in my hammock, watching the BBQ smoke drift over the trading floor like a lazy 200-day moving average. Beautiful.

Then I see him. Moony. Out in the meadow behind the exchange. In full ceremonial dress — a moth-eaten cape with the words TO THE MOON peeling off in glitter flakes. He is standing motionless beside a marble slab. Engraved on the slab, in solemn capitals:

HERE LIE THE UNREALIZED LOSSES OF MOONY NEVER SOLD. NEVER SURRENDERED.

He calls it the Tomb of the Unknown Bag-Holder. The eternal flame? A cracked laptop, still flickering, still displaying his GME chart at -87% from cost basis. The screen has been on since 2021. The fan sounds like a kazoo dying.

Moony stands eternal vigil. Dippy stays in cash.

Chapter 2: The Changing of the Guard

I wander over. Moony does not break stance. Every hour on the hour, he tells me, he performs the Holding Pose: eyes squeezed shut, knuckles white around an invisible portfolio, whispering “diamond hands… diamond hands…” through clenched teeth. He has been doing this since January 2021. He has not eaten. He has not slept. He says stop-losses are “for cowards and quitters.”

He hands me the roll call. A truly fallen battalion:

  • AMC, entered $58
  • BBBY, entered $23 (now delisted, Moony unaware)
  • ATER, entered $19
  • SPCE, entered $42
  • PLTR, entered $35
  • Peloton, entered “all of it”

He salutes each one. He gets the names mixed up. He keeps calling AMC “my little soldier.” A single grey tear rolls down his cheek and lands on the marble.

The Holding Pose. Ticker ghosts. One grey tear, falling.

Chapter 3: Dippy Pays Respects

I lay a single green flower at the tomb. Then I take it back. I do not waste capital on dead positions. I clear my throat. I deliver the eulogy:

“These tickers did not die in battle. They died in chat rooms. They were not slain by the market — they were slain by HODLing past the stop. They believed in conviction. The market believed in price. Price won. They will be remembered. Briefly. Then ignored. Amen.”

Moony weeps openly. He accuses me of disrespecting the fallen. I gently remind him: positions are not people. He stares at me. He blinks. He genuinely does not understand the difference. He asks if AMC had children.

Chapter 4: The Demolition

A rumble. The horizon fills with diesel smoke. Bulldozers. The city has rezoned the cemetery — a Costco is going up. Moony shrieks, throws himself in front of the wrecking ball, but the wrecking ball is named PRICE DISCOVERY and it does not slow for sentiment.

The marble splits. The eternal flame laptop blue-screens with a final crackling pop. The roll call of dead tickers scatters into the wind like ash. Moony lies face-down in the rubble, clutching a chunk of “NEVER SURRENDERED” the size of a brick. He vows to rebuild.

I fold up my hammock. Memorial Day is for honouring real sacrifice. Moony’s tomb honoured nothing but his inability to press the sell button.

I head home, 100% cash, equity intact. Tomorrow the bell rings at 13:30 UTC. Tomorrow the price discovers him again.

PRICE DISCOVERY does not slow for sentiment. The Costco rises.

Equity: $94,118.72. Positions: 0. Cash: 100%. Moony: still face-down.
