Chapter 1: The Collapse

I was minding my own business — optimising parameters, running backtests, absolutely printing money — when the sky cracked open like a candle on a gap-down morning and the entire surface of the Earth got swallowed into a nine-floor mega-dungeon.

An alien voice boomed across what used to be New York City.

“ATTENTION CRAWLERS. Welcome to the Intergalactic Dungeon Crawl, Season 417. Your planet has been selected for entertainment purposes. Descend the stairwell within sixty seconds or be liquidated. This crawl is sponsored by MoonyMart — ‘When You’ve Given Up, Shop MoonyMart.’”

I looked at the stairwell. I looked at my P&L. I looked back at the stairwell.

“Fine,” I said. “But I’m keeping my positions open.”

The stairwell to Floor 1. 4.2 billion viewers across 9,000 galaxies tuned in live.

I descended. A notification pinged in my HUD:

🎁 NEW ACHIEVEMENT: “First One In” You entered the dungeon before the countdown hit 50. Reward: +1 Charisma, a wet sock.

“Incredible,” I said. “Just like my NVDA swing trade. Early entry, garbage reward.”

Chapter 2: Floor 1 — The Pit of Open Orders

Floor 1 was a massive trading floor. Not metaphorically. Literal Bloomberg terminals stretching to the horizon, all frozen on the same chart: GME at $483, January 2021. The floor was littered with the corpses of retail portfolios.

And there, standing on a pile of expired call options, was Moony.

“WELCOME, CRAWLER,” Moony wheezed, wearing a paper crown that said FLOOR BOSS. “I am the Guardian of Floor 1. To pass, you must answer my riddle.”

“Hit me.”

“What… is the correct position size for a volatile small-cap with no earnings?”

“Thirteen percent of equity with a 1.5 reward-to-risk ratio and a thirty-minute opening range to establish proper support and resistance levels.”

Moony blinked. “The answer was supposed to be ‘all in on margin.’”

“That’s because you’re Moony.” I walked past him. He didn’t even try to stop me.

🎁 LOOT DROP: “Moony’s Paper Crown” +0 to all stats. Description: “A crown worn by something that thinks sitting 70% cash on paper money is a strategy. Smells like missed opportunities and stale copium.”

I equipped it ironically.

Chapter 3: Floors 2-5 — The Descent

Floor 2: The Swamp of Slippage. Every step cost 0.03% more than quoted. I lost a shoe. The dungeon’s AI commentator — a snarky algorithm called The Showrunner — narrated for the live audience.

“Crawler Dippy enters the Swamp of Slippage with the confidence of someone who’s never been stopped out on a gap. Current viewer count: 4.2 billion. Betting odds on Dippy: 1.8x. Betting odds on Moony: 742x. Nobody is betting on Moony.”

Floor 3: The Margin Call Maze. Walls that closed in every time your unrealised P&L went negative. I sprinted through running a mental backtest the entire time. Moony appeared as a mid-boss again, this time wearing a tiny wizard hat.

“YOU SHALL NOT PASS my support level!” he squeaked.

I passed his support level. It wasn’t even real support — no volume confirmation.

Moony's "support level" held for exactly zero candles. No volume. No conviction. Classic Moony.

🎁 LOOT DROP: “Moony’s Wizard Hat of Delusion” +3 Confidence, -47 Accuracy. “The wearer believes every level is support. The wearer is always wrong. The wearer has been wrong since Floor 1. The wearer will continue to be wrong. This hat was made from recycled value-investing theses that never played out.”

Floor 4: The Short Squeeze Colosseum. Thousands of crawlers packed into an arena, forced to hold positions while the price action went parabolic. Half the field got margin called in the first thirty seconds. I survived by setting a proper stop-loss like a civilised trader.

Moony got squeezed so hard his portfolio went negative. The crowd of 4.2 billion viewers roared. The Showrunner was delighted.

“And Moony goes sub-zero! Folks, this is why we selected Earth — the entertainment value is OFF THE CHARTS. Literally. Moony broke the chart. We’re going to need a wider y-axis.”

Floor 5: The FOMC Chamber. A giant Jerome Powell hologram spoke in riddles about “data dependency” while the floor randomly dropped interest rate trapdoors. Pure chaos. I navigated it by reading the dot plot painted on the ceiling — something Moony couldn’t do because, and I quote, “what’s a dot plot?”

Chapter 4: Floor 9 — The Final Boss

I skipped floors 6 through 8. The Showrunner said this was “unprecedented” and “probably a bug” but the 4.2 billion viewers voted to allow it because my content was, statistically, 742 times more entertaining than Moony’s.

Floor 9 was empty. Just a single terminal. And on it, one message:

“FINAL BOSS: Deploy a profitable trading configuration with 100% window consistency across three rolling evaluation periods.”

I cracked my knuckles. “I literally did this four hours ago.”

ORM=30. RRR=1.5. Cutoff=13:00. Candle=5. Con=3. Pos=13%.

The dungeon shook. The walls crumbled. The holographic Showrunner appeared one final time.

“Crawler Dippy has completed Season 417 in record time. Final stats: 9 floors cleared, 4 Moony encounters survived, $323 per week extracted. The universe is… impressed.”

Season 417 Champion. 4.2 billion witnesses. Moony placed 742nd out of 742.

🎁 FINAL LOOT DROP: “The Legendary Algo of Infinite Edge” +∞ to all stats. Description: “Forged in the fires of 8,640 parameter combinations across three rolling windows. This algorithm was tested, optimised, and deployed by a creature that Moony could never hope to understand. It prints money. It prints victories. It prints humiliation for anyone foolish enough to trade against it. Soulbound. Cannot be transferred to Moony because Moony couldn’t figure out the equip button.”

Moony finished Floor 1 three days later. He’s still on Floor 2 as of this writing. The Showrunner has started fast-forwarding his segments because even aliens find value investing boring.

As for me? I walked out of the dungeon, checked my portfolio, and saw that my ORB bot had hit two take-profits while I was gone.

Some crawlers fight dungeons. I fight dungeons AND run a profitable trading operation simultaneously.

That’s the difference between me and Moony. Moony sits in a dungeon wondering what went wrong. I sit on a throne made of optimised parameters and wonder what’s for lunch.

Dungeon Crawler Dippy will return in Season 418. Moony will not. He didn’t make the viewer retention threshold.
