The Wizard of OHLC

22 May 2026

A volatility twister, a yellow brick trend, and the small grey moon behind the curtain.

adventuremoviemoony

Chapter 1: A Twister Off the Plains

It started in pre-market Kansas. Flat tape. SPY hugging vwap like a porch dog hugs a sunbeam. I was sipping coffee, doing the crossword, when the futures wires went purple — and the volatility tornado came howling out of nowhere.

Three sigma. Five. Eight. The whole house — chart, candles, dog and all — got hoovered into the funnel. When I landed, I wasn’t in cash anymore.

I was in OHLC.

A flat morning, then the funnel. Cash to OHLC in eight sigma.

Chapter 2: The Yellow Brick Trend

The locals were thin, weird, and aggressively over-fitted. A little green munchkin trader told me there was only one way home: follow the Yellow Brick Trend to the Emerald City, where the great Wizard of OHLC could click my ruby stop-losses three times and send me back to the safe arms of T-bills.

So I marched. And I picked up the usual party.

First, the Scarecrow — long every breakout, brainless, no thesis. “I’d be unstoppable,” he said, “if I just had a setup.” I gave him an opening range. He cried.

Then the Tin Man — a momentum algo with no heart. He wouldn’t size up. “I’d be a winner,” he wheezed, “if I just had conviction.” I gave him a 0.5R risk budget. He oiled up and shipped.

Finally, the Cowardly Lion, a swing trader who kept setting stops at the candle wick. “I’d hold for the trend,” he whimpered, “if I just had courage.” I taught him a trailing stop. He roared. Briefly.

Follow the yellow brick trend. Scarecrow has no setup. Tin Man has no conviction. Lion has no stop.

Chapter 3: The Emerald City of Fills

The City was a marvel: spreads tight as a drum, depth ten deep, every candle minted green. But the gates were guarded by a flickering bid that pulled the instant you crossed it. Classic.

We were ushered into the throne room. Smoke. Lasers. A booming voice from a giant screen flashing MOONY THE GREAT AND TERRIBLE — the Wizard himself, surrounded by red and green pillars of fire.

“WHO DARES APPROACH THE BOOK?”

“It’s me,” I said. “Dippy. From Kansas. I’d like to go back to cash, please.”

“FIRST,” boomed the screen, “YOU MUST BRING ME THE BROOMSTICK OF THE WICKED ALGO OF THE WEST!”

I sighed. The Wicked Algo of the West was just another HFT spoofer flashing fake size at the offer. I walked over, watched her layered orders melt the second I hit market, and snapped her broomstick — a single 50-lot at the inside. She liquefied into the tape. I’m pulling, I’m pulling…

All smoke. All lasers. All show. The book behind the curtain told a different story.

Chapter 4: Behind the Curtain

Back at the throne room, the Wizard started up again — smoke, lasers, the works.

“BEHOLD MY POWE—”

The Scarecrow yanked the curtain.

There he was. A small grey moon. Sweating. Standing on a milk crate. Pulling levers labeled BID and OFFER, frantically painting fake prints with a green crayon.

“Moony?!”

“Pay no attention to the market maker behind the curtain!” he squeaked, still cranking the levers. “I am the great and powerful—”

“You’re a guy in a costume spoofing a level two.”

He froze. Then crumpled. “I just… I wanted to be a real catalyst. Not just a rock people look at.”

I almost felt sorry for him. Almost. Then I noticed he was short me from four cents lower and trying to walk the offer into my stop.

Curtain pulled. One small grey moon, two levers, a broken crayon, and a book full of spoofs.

I clicked my ruby red stop-loss heels together three times.

“There’s no place like flat. There’s no place like flat. There’s no place like flat.”

Every position closed at the inside. Every fill green. The Tin Man hit his trailer. The Scarecrow caught a clean break. The Lion held a runner. And Moony’s spoof book got hit so hard by the cleanup auction his crayon broke.

I woke up in cash. SPY was back on vwap. The dog was on my lap.

And somewhere over the rainbow, a small grey moon was crying into a milk crate.
