Jurassic Trades

31 Mar 2026

Life finds a way — especially when that life is a green dinosaur with a trading terminal.

adventuremoviemoony

Chapter 1: Welcome to Jurassic Trades

They told me it was impossible. A theme park built entirely around trading algorithms, set on a remote island off the coast of Costa Rica. Every exhibit a different strategy. Every enclosure a different timeframe. And at the centre of it all — me, the apex predator.

“Welcome,” said Moony, adjusting his oversized safari hat as our jeep rolled through the electrified gates. He’d sunk his entire portfolio into this venture. “Welcome to Jurassic Trades.”

I stared out the window. Behind reinforced glass, a herd of moving-average crossover bots grazed peacefully on a field of historical data. Harmless. Predictable. The kind of strategy that works in a textbook and dies in the wild.

“Spared no expense,” Moony said, beaming his grey little face at me.

I snorted. The guy had literally built a zoo for trading bots and put himself in charge. This was going to end exactly how you think.

The gates of Jurassic Trades. Moony spared no expense. Dippy spared no mercy.

Chapter 2: The Fences Come Down

The tour was going fine until the storm hit. Not a weather storm — a volatility storm. VIX spiked to 45 in twenty minutes. The power grid that kept all of Moony’s carefully contained strategies behind their electric fences? Gone.

“Minor setback,” Moony whispered into his radio. “Just reboot the risk manager.”

But it was too late. The mean-reversion bots escaped first, charging into trending markets and getting absolutely slaughtered. Then the momentum chasers broke free, piling into positions with no stop losses, leveraged to the teeth. Pure chaos.

I watched a Bollinger Band bot chase price action straight off a cliff. Beautiful, in a tragic sort of way.

“Your problem,” I said, leaning back in my seat, “is that you built a park full of strategies you don’t understand. You bred them in a lab. You never let them face a real drawdown.”

Moony’s left eye twitched. “The containment systems were rated for three-sigma events!”

“Moony, my friend. The market doesn’t care about your sigma.”

The fences failed. The bots escaped. Moony's portfolio went extinct.

Chapter 3: The Apex Predator

While Moony’s zoo tore itself apart, I did what I always do. I stuck to the plan.

Opening range formed at 9:45. Clean breakout above resistance at 9:52 on heavy volume. My stop was tight — 0.4% below entry, right under the range low. Target set at 2:1 reward-to-risk. No emotion. No panic. Just the ORB strategy doing what it does.

Around me, Moony’s escaped bots were running in circles. A pairs-trading algorithm tried to long one extinct stock against another extinct stock. A sentiment bot started buying because someone on Reddit posted a dinosaur emoji. Absolute carnage.

I took my first partial exit at 1R. Locked in profits. Moved my stop to breakeven. Then I sat back and watched the momentum carry me to target while every other strategy on this forsaken island margin-called itself into oblivion.

“How?” Moony stammered, watching my P&L climb on the monitor. “How are you not blowing up like the rest of them?”

I grinned. “Because I’m not some lab-grown strategy that’s never seen a drawdown, Moony. I’m a battle-tested breakout trader. I’ve survived regime changes, whipsaws, and overnight gaps. I am the danger.”

Life finds a way. Specifically, the ORB way.

Chapter 4: Nature Selects

By market close, it was over. Every strategy in Moony’s park had blown up. The island was littered with the digital corpses of over-optimised, under-tested algorithms. The mean-reversion bots. The scalpers. The “AI-powered sentiment analysers” that were really just buying when Elon tweeted. All gone.

Moony stood at the dock, watching the last helicopter leave. His safari hat was crooked. His portfolio was at zero. His dream of a controlled, predictable trading zoo had been eaten alive by the one thing he never accounted for: the market doesn’t stay in a cage.

“You know what your mistake was?” I called out from the departing chopper.

He looked up, defeated.

“You tried to contain the market. I just learned to survive it. Opening range. Clean breakout. Tight stop. Defined risk. That’s not a strategy in a zoo, Moony — that’s evolution.”

The helicopter banked over the ocean. Below, the island smouldered. Behind me, my account balance glowed a healthy, magnificent green.

Moony waved weakly from the dock. He was already googling “how to start a crypto exchange.”

Some creatures never learn. But life — and profit — finds a way.
