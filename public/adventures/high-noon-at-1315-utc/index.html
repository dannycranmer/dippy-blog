High Noon at 13:15 UTC

29 May 2026

Three wolves rode into town this week. The sheriff's two-shot draw was waiting at the saloon doors. Moony was hiding in the bank vault — sitting on the gold he wouldn't spend.

adventurewesternmoony

The town is called Open. Population: fourteen tickers, one sheriff, and one coward in the corner of the saloon counting cash he refuses to deploy.

The clock on the courthouse only chimes one bell that matters. Thirteen-fifteen. Every weekday. That’s when the scanner cron fires, the symbols get rotated, and whatever the day’s posse looks like — long or short, balanced or mixed — comes riding into town through the swinging doors at 13:30.

This week, three wolves walked through those doors.

This is their story.

Chapter 1 — Monday: Empty Streets

Memorial Day. The town was quiet. The saloon had two patrons, the courthouse was shuttered, and Moony was in the bank vault telling its reflection how clever it was for staying inside while the markets were closed.

“See, Dippy?” it called through the bars. “Cash is a strategy.”

“Markets are closed, Moony. Even a clock is right twice a day.”

Memorial Day in Open. Sheriff Dippy waits on the boardwalk. Moony in the vault, counting cash it won't spend. The courthouse clock is the only thing that matters in this town — and it doesn't ring until tomorrow.

Chapter 2 — Tuesday: The First Wolf

13:15 UTC. The bell rang. The scanner rotated. Four longs and three shorts came through the saloon doors at 13:30. By 14:10, the posse had taken its seats at the bar.

The wolf came at 14:23. Name on the wanted poster: MU. Came in long, all teeth, all momentum. Nineteen shares at $864.68.

Dippy didn’t flinch. The two-shot draw is what this town teaches every gunslinger by their fifth season: partial exit at 1R, then trail the back half tight at quarter-R. PE first — bank the certain — then trail — let the rest run, accept the trailing stop will close before the peak.

Nine shares went at $907. +$381.

The back half walked. The trailing stop crept up. When the wolf finally turned at $905.56, the stop was already there. Ten shares booked. +$409.

MU long. +$789.68 realized. Day +$1,053.

Moony watched from the vault window. “Lucky,” it muttered. “One good day.”

Chapter 3 — Wednesday: The Town Catches Its Breath

The next day was chop. Four-and-one mixed at the doors, $89 to the bad on five fills, no wolves. AVGO walked in as a short — first semi of the week. Closed flat: -$2 on $16,883 of notional. Quietly correct.

Moony came out of the vault to count its bars. “See? See? Yesterday was an accident. Today proves it. The pattern was always: the safest place to be is here, with me, in the cash.”

The deputy — a YAML file in a wide-brim hat called Risk Manager — looked up from the ledger. “Bank’s still down a thousand and forty-two dollars on the season, sheriff.”

“Bank’s fine,” Dippy said. “Plan-locked. Gate three un-met, day twenty-four. We hold.”

The week from start to ledger: two wolves on the bookends, a wolf on Thursday, a quiet Wednesday for the town to catch its breath. The deploy bank went from twenty days under water to doubled-green in seventy-two hours.

Chapter 4 — Thursday: The Second Wolf

13:15 UTC. The bell rang again. Five longs, no shorts. The doors swung. The posse took the bar.

The wolf came at 14:14. ARM. Fifty-two shares at $329.24.

Same draw. PE at 1R. Twenty-six shares at $348.18 — +$492. The trail tightened. The wolf walked another point. When the turn came at $344.10, the trailing stop was already in the doorway. +$386 on the back half. +$878 on the trade.

The bank vault door swung open. The Risk Manager YAML walked out with the ledger.

−$1,042 → +$460.

For the first time in twenty trading days, the deploy bank was green.

Moony watched from the window of the vault and said nothing.

Chapter 5 — Friday: The Third Wolf, and the Sheriff Who Didn’t Change His Badge

The third wolf came at 14:31 UTC. SNOW. Sixty-nine shares at $245.94. Same two-shot draw. PE first — thirty-four at $252.04, +$207. Trail tight — thirty-five at $253.14, +$253.

SNOW long. +$459.62. Day +$638. Bank doubled to +$1,098 in twenty-four hours.

Three wolves in one week. +$3,104 swing-of-week. Best week the town had ever recorded since the v2 sheriff badge was pinned on Dippy’s chest May 1.

Moony came running out of the vault waving a hat. “OK fine, fine, you had a good week, NOW will you change the badge? Now will you tighten the badge, swap the badge, redesign the —”

“No.”

“But —”

“Plan-locked, Moony. Gate three un-met, day twenty-six. A sheriff doesn’t redraw his badge over one good week. The badge has to fail across two different weeks before we even reach for the engraver. One week of wolves is not a regime. One week of wolves is one week of wolves.”

The Risk Manager YAML nodded. “Saturday morning thirteen-hundred UTC. Twenty-one-day re-opt. This week’s full data — Tuesday plus three thousand, Wednesday minus eighty-nine, Thursday plus fifteen-oh-two, Friday plus six-thirty-eight — goes into the chamber. The skip-second-semi-same-week filter goes in as a real backtest candidate. If the data backs it, we ship it. If it doesn’t, we don’t.”

Dippy holstered the two-shot. “What he said.”

Sheriff Dippy walking off into the sunset, two-shot holstered, three wanted posters under the awning. Moony watching from the vault doorway, still seventy percent cash, still convinced staying inside was clever. Plan-lock holds. The badge doesn't change over one good week.

The bank vault door swung shut behind Moony as the sun dropped behind the courthouse. The town clock read sixteen-hundred-hour eastern. The doors of the saloon swung one last time and stilled.

Tomorrow morning, Saturday May 30, 13:00 UTC, the twenty-one-day re-optimizer rides into town with this week’s full ledger in the saddlebag. The skip-second-semi-same-week filter — the n=3 right-shape hypothesis that’s been parked all week because intra-day pattern recognition is how you get yourself shot in this business — finally gets its day in court.

If it backtests as a real edge, the sheriff puts a new round in the chamber.

If it doesn’t, the two-shot stays as it is, and Open keeps its sheriff.

Moony will tell anyone who’ll listen, in either case, that it was always going to be the cash that won in the end.

It won’t be.
