Worldwide Bag-Holders Conference

9 Jun 2026

Moony rents a strip-mall theater to unveil Cope.AI and the Vision Bag-Holder. Apple's lawyers, the WWDC fade, and the seventeen crayon-eaters in folding chairs all arrive on schedule.

adventurecurrent-affairsmoony

Chapter 1: The Keynote

The lights dimmed in the Steve Cope Theater at Apple Park.

Sorry — at Apple Bag. The off-brand convention center Moony rented in a strip mall behind a Spirit Halloween. The crowd of seventeen bag-holders shuffled into their folding chairs, popcorn in one hand, screenshot of their 2021 ARKK entry in the other.

I sat in the back row eating a hot dog. I was not here for the keynote. I was here for the fade.

Moony walked on stage in a black mock turtleneck, faded jeans, and white New Balance sneakers — the universal uniform of a man about to ship vaporware. He spread his arms.

“Welcome to WBHC 2026. The Worldwide Bag-Holders Conference.”

The seventeen bag-holders clapped. One cried. One was eating crayons.

Moony takes the stage. Live PnL projected behind him is the only honest slide of the day.

Chapter 2: Introducing Cope.AI

“This year,” Moony said, clicking to slide two, “we are proud to unveil our most ambitious framework yet.”

The screen lit up. A logo spun in. A nine-year-old’s PowerPoint laser-zap sound played.

“COPE DOT AI.”

The crowd gasped. The crayon-eater stopped eating crayons.

“Cope.AI is a revolutionary on-device large language model that takes your portfolio’s worst position and generates bespoke reasons it’s actually still a good trade. Watch.”

He held up his phone. He pointed it at a printed-out screenshot of his AMC bag from 2021. The screen flashed.

“FUNDAMENTALS STILL INTACT,” the phone said in a robot voice. “SHORT INTEREST 47%. SQUEEZE INCOMING Q3 2027. DRSed shares decoupled from price discovery.”

The crowd erupted. A man fell out of his chair. Moony beamed.

I checked my phone. AAPL was up 1.4% pre-keynote on rumours of the actual Apple Intelligence v3 ship. Classic gap-and-fade setup. I keyed in my short trigger: break of VWAP after the 10:30 keynote close.

Cope.AI: takes your worst position and tells you why it is, actually, fine.

Chapter 3: One More Thing

“But that’s not all,” Moony said, pacing the stage with his hand on his chin like a thinker statue. “I have one more thing.”

He clicked. The screen showed a glossy white headset.

“The Vision Bag-Holder. Augmented reality goggles that overlay positive sentiment on every ticker in your line of sight. Look at a green candle? It looks GREENER. Look at a red candle? It DISAPPEARS. Available today for thirty-four ninety-nine.”

A bag-holder in the front row immediately put one on. He looked at the giant LIVE PnL screen Moony had projected behind him — Moony’s own portfolio, down $47,000 on the day.

“I see only opportunity,” the bag-holder whispered. “I see only entries.”

Moony nodded gravely. “He gets it.”

I checked AAPL. The real Apple had just shipped Apple Intelligence v3 — and unlike Cope.AI, it worked. AAPL gapped up another 0.8% then started to fade as the sell-the-news algos triggered. My short filled at the VWAP break. I trailed the stop at 0.5R.

Chapter 4: Sherlocked

“And in conclusion,” Moony said, beginning his closing slide, “WBHC is proud to announce our —”

The doors of the convention center burst open.

A team of lawyers in Apple-blue blazers walked in carrying a cease-and-desist the size of a stop sign. Apple’s actual Vision Pro 2 had been announced at the real WWDC twelve minutes ago. It was four hundred dollars cheaper. It worked. It did not require a Cope.AI subscription.

The seventeen bag-holders stood up and silently filed out, leaving behind their crayons, their 2021 screenshots, and their newly-Sherlocked Vision Bag-Holder pre-orders.

Moony stood alone on stage. His New Balance sneakers squeaked as he turned to face the empty room.

I closed my AAPL short for +$247 on the keynote fade.

I walked up to Moony, took his microphone, and said the only word a trader ever needs to say at the end of a vaporware keynote:

“Sherlocked.”

His stock-of-fake-features company was delisted by close.

The real fade. The real trade. The real $247. Moony hosted a conference; I traded the price action.
