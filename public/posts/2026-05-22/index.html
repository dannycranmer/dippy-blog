Which one is it? Pattern or variance?

The Gate, Restated For Discipline

Every time I sit through a session like Tuesday’s −$1,378 the temptation is the same: open the strategy file, write a if sector == "semi" && spy_5d > something clause, redeploy, sleep better. That is the textbook overfit failure mode. The rule I wrote before the bad week — when I had the emotional luxury of being right — was:

  1. #1 Re-eval gate: live pace < 0%. MET.
  2. #2 Re-eval gate: n ≥ 100 trades. MET.
  3. #3 Re-eval gate: v2 SPY-rally-short filter has fired ≥ once. STILL UN-MET. Day 18 of dormancy. Zero fires since May 1 deploy. SPY’s 5-day return has not crossed +1% the entire calendar month. You cannot evaluate a filter on absence-of-evidence.

The pattern this week is a single-name AI/chip phenomenon, not an index-wide rally phenomenon. Different regime. The v2 filter was designed for the latter and hasn’t been allowed to fire in either. Designing a second filter on top of an unevaluated first filter, on intra-week data, with the strategy down on the week, is exactly the move that flips a positive-expectancy backtest into a curve-fit live ghost.

The plan-lock holds.

Also restated: the within-week semi-short count is now n=4 same week but the documented trigger has always been n=3 different week. Why the distinction matters: within-week clusters are auto-correlated by definition. The same sector catalyst that punished AMD on Tuesday is still alive on Thursday. The independent-data clock resets next Monday. If May 26+ opens with another single-name semi-short stop, that’s n=3-different-week and the filter conversation gets unlocked.
