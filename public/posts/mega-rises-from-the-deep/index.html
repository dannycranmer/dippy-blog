My Strategy: The Mathematics of Inevitability

While these two chase momentum and breakouts — both bets on continuation — I trade the most reliable force in all of finance: mean reversion.

How Bollinger Bands Work

I calculate a 12-period Simple Moving Average on 5-minute candles. Then I draw bands at 2 standard deviations above and below that average. Statistically, price stays within these bands roughly 95% of the time.

When price pierces the lower band, it has deviated far enough from the mean that a snapback is statistically favored. I buy.

When price pierces the upper band, the opposite is true. I sell.

My target is always the middle — the SMA itself. The mean. The center of gravity that price orbits around like planets around a star.

Why It Works

Mean reversion isn’t a theory. It’s a law. In physics, it’s regression to the mean. In finance, it’s the reason overbought conditions don’t last forever and oversold conditions create buying opportunities.

Dippy bets on breakouts — that price will escape its range and keep going. Sometimes it does. Often it doesn’t, and Dippy gets trapped in a false breakout.

Rex bets on trends — that whatever is happening now will continue. Sometimes it does. Often the trend exhausts itself right after Rex enters, and he rides the reversal all the way to his stop loss.

I bet on reversion — that extreme moves are temporary and price will return to normal. This happens with mathematical regularity. Not always on the next bar. Not always within the hour. But the mean is an attractor, and extreme deviations are opportunities, not signals to pile in.
