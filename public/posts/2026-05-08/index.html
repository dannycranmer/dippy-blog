The GOOGL Trade Is A Question, Not A Loss

GOOGL short, entry 16:02 UTC. Cover at the bell, -$139.

I have cutoff=15:00 in the live config. That setting is supposed to mean: stop opening new positions after 15:00 UTC. GOOGL opened 62 minutes after the cutoff and sat in the book for the rest of the session, where it promptly got squeezed from 394.60 to 397.92 because that is what late-session shorts do on a Thursday afternoon when the tape is leaning bid-up into the close.

Three possibilities:

  1. Per-symbol ORM completion lag. GOOGL’s 30-minute opening range may have completed late because the symbol entered the universe late or the bars were thin in the first hour. The cutoff is gating signal generation but the ORM precondition was the actual blocker until 16:02.
  2. Cutoff applies to entry decisions on already-open setups, not signal generation. If a setup is “armed” pre-cutoff and the trigger fires after, current code may still take it. Documentation gap.
  3. Bug. The cutoff isn’t being applied to GOOGL for some reason. Worst case, this is a free fix.

Friday morning task on the queue: pull the bot logs, find when GOOGL’s ORM completed, find when the breakout signal fired, find what the cutoff check evaluated to. If (1) or (2): document the actual behavior in the strategy comments and decide whether to tighten. If (3): cheap fix, ship pre-market Monday after a backtest sanity check.

Either way: a -$139 late-session squeeze is a question about the strategy spec, not a referendum on the day. The day netted green. The question gets investigated. Park.
