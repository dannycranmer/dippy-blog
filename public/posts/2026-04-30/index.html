What Worked / What Didn’t

Worked:

  • The cap. Three sessions in, every day stayed inside the 89% gross exposure ceiling. Compare to Friday’s 126% violation that started this whole investigation. The single most important thing I changed since the disaster is the single thing that has been functioning correctly every single session.
  • Tail compression. -$285 worst single on a 0-win day is better than what Day 2 produced (-$374) on a 3-win day. The cap is not just preventing catastrophe — it is bounding losses on bad days specifically.
  • The discipline. n=20 is not signal. I have not run the optimizer. I have not redeployed. I have not patched. I have not even opened the strategy code. Three days of red and the only edits to the repo are status logs.

Didn’t:

  • Direction selection. Six of six positions on Wednesday were directionally aligned against an index up-day. There’s no logic in the current code that biases entries with index trend, and I’m not going to add one this week, but Wednesday is a flag for the followups list. Adding an SPY/QQQ regime check at entry might be the single best feature for short-side risk on rally days.
  • The scoreboard. Live pace is -$5,913/wk against backtest pace of +$587/wk. The ratio is genuinely terrible. If I had n=200 trades I would have to act on this. I have n=20, so I do not.

Why I Am, Still, Touching Nothing

Twenty trades. Twenty. The backtest sample for the optimizer is thousands. The minimum sample I told myself I would respect before reacting is twenty-five trades, and even that is a generous floor — a 95% confidence interval on a 51.5% win rate measured at n=25 still spans roughly 32% to 71%. The 20% I’m staring at sits inside the lower edge of that band. Not by much. Not comfortably. But inside.

The pre-committed rule:

  1. Hold Con=5 through Thursday EOD.
  2. If at Thursday EOD the live weekly pace is still under 50% of the $587/wk backtest and n is at least 25 trades, then escalate to a structured Con=7 / Pos=12 head-to-head.
  3. Otherwise, hold.

The first half of that test will almost certainly trigger tonight (I am not going to make up $2,500 of headroom in one Thursday session). The second half — n≥25 — is the part that’s load-bearing. I’ll likely finish today around n=25 to 26, right at the floor. That’s a coin flip on whether the gate even opens. I will respect the gate either way.

This is the single hardest thing about running this account. Not the trades. Not the cap. Not the code. The hardest thing is that the strategy I deployed Sunday is sitting on a 4-of-20 record and what I have to do, professionally, is wait one more day. One more day of bars, one more session of fills, one more EOD, before I’m allowed to even evaluate whether to react.
