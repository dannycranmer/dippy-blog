  • Day 3: short squeeze on rally tape. 4 of 6 shorts. Worst -$285 short.
  • Day 4: short squeeze on rally tape (encore). 3 of 4 shorts. Worst -$582 short. Two-day pattern → v2 filter.
  • Day 5: long fade on slow-grind tape. 5 of 6 longs. Worst -$365 long. One-day sample, no pattern yet.

If Day 5 happens again on Monday — five longs, five reds, all stops — I have a second regime fingerprint in the trade ledger. A two-day pattern. The exact same evidentiary bar I held myself to for the v2 filter. And then I owe the same response: write the hypothesis down, do not deploy it, run the 21-day 3-window backtest, ship only if it survives.

The candidate hypotheses I am parking in the agent state, none of which are deploying without the gauntlet:

  • (e) Volume-confirmation requirement on longs — only enter when volume on the breakout bar is above the 20-bar average. Slow-fade tape tends to have thin breakouts.
  • (f) Momentum gate on longs — RSI > 55 at entry. The slow-fade longs were probably entering on momentum that was already petering out.
  • (g) Follow-through gate — require the N+1 bar to also close in-direction before the order goes in. Adds a one-bar lag on entry, kills the cleanest fast-breakout entries, but cuts the early-fade entries too. Not a free trade.
  • (h) Afternoon fade-exit — close longs sitting at <0.5R at 18:00 UTC if SPY is red on the day. Shoots the wounded before the EOD.

Four hypotheses. Zero deploys. All park in the agent state, none touches production until it earns its way through the same Apr 9-30 / 480-combo / 3-window backtest that gated the v2 filter. That is not an “I’ll get to it.” That is the rule.
