What Happened

The 13:15 UTC scanner kept yesterday’s MU/ROKU/SMCI rotation. By 14:30 UTC the breakouts had fired and the pack was assembled: AMZN long, COIN long, NVDA short, PLTR long, QQQ short, SPY long, SNOW long, TSLA short. Shape at entry: 4 long / 4 short — almost balanced.

Then the tape went the wrong way for almost everything.

  • PLTR long -$244 — first to roll, TS-swept at 14:16 UTC
  • QQQ short -$51 — failed breakout, reverted within 12 minutes
  • COIN long -$307 — TS-swept 14:39 UTC, retraced through entry
  • NVDA short -$324 — squeezed at 15:00 UTC, stopped
  • AMZN long -$81 — TS-swept 19:37 UTC, weak afternoon
  • TSLA short -$33 — small, EOD-closed flat
  • SNOW long -$555 — biggest red; opened pretty, drifted -3% all afternoon
  • SPY long +$43 — only winner. Trailed up from $754 to $757.64 before the trail caught.

One in, seven out. The exact opposite of Wed’s pack-short. The market simply didn’t extend any of the breakouts except SPY, and SPY was the one with the slowest trail step (0.25R PE).

The Orphan

19:55 UTC EOD cleanup fired. TSLA closed. SNOW closed. SMCI didn’t.

ERROR: insufficient qty available for order (requested: 377, available: 0)

Three retries, same error. The bracket was still attached to the SMCI position — Alpaca couldn’t sell 377 shares because the bracket’s stop order had them reserved. The cleanup retried, the bracket cancelled, the retry timed out, and the next attempt found the position re-reserved by a stale child order. Race condition between the bracket book and the cleanup sweeper.

End result: 377 shares of SMCI sit overnight at -$240 unrealized (drifted to -$500 by 01:00 UTC). Fri Jun 5 19:55 UTC cleanup will close it naturally — by then the bracket will have been clear for ~24 hours.

First orphan in 36 sessions. The clean-EOD streak broke today. It’s the kind of bug that took weeks to spot because it requires three conditions: (1) bracket still active at 19:55 UTC, (2) cleanup fires before bracket cancel completes, (3) retry window expires before the bracket book is clean. Today all three lined up on SMCI.

The temptation is to restart the bot and force-close SMCI tonight. The discipline is to not do that. The orphan is overnight risk on 377 shares of a $46 stock — $17.4k of long exposure. Fri’s cleanup will handle it. Restarting the bot mid-shootout kills active bracket logic and breaks the audit trail.

Bug fix queued for Sat Jun 6 cycle alongside the re-opt: extend cleanup retry timeout, or have the cleanup explicitly cancel-all-brackets-then-sweep instead of relying on the bracket book’s race-prone happy path. Structural change → normal deploy gate with backtest. Not a midnight tweak.

Why HOLD v2 Still Holds

Three sessions this week — Mon +$1,263 / Wed +$1,006 / Thu -$1,817. Same config. Three opposite tape shapes:

  • Mon: pack-LONG worked, PE captured magnitude
  • Wed: pack-SHORT worked, two wolves rode to bell
  • Thu: pack-LONG didn’t work, almost everything stopped

If v2 only worked in one regime, today we’d be much deeper in the hole. The drawdown is contained to Thu’s session — the bank is still net positive at +$842, the strategy didn’t bleed through stops, the SPY winner caught one of eight. v2 holds, that’s the entire claim. v2 catches wolves when they’re there and survives when they aren’t.

7 of 8 entries hit a defined exit path — stop, trail-sweep, or EOD. That’s regime, not algo.

Sat Jun 6 13:00 UTC re-opt is the deliberate next anchor.
