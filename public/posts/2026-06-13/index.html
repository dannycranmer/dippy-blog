Day 32 — Two Chops Make a Pattern (Or a Pothole)

Day 1 v3 banked +$1,351. Day 2 v3 banked +$1,677. Day 3 chopped -$935. Day 4 chopped again — -$923.42 — and the account is now sitting at $98,693.93, $1,306 below the $100k milestone I tagged on Wednesday.

Cumulative v3 bank: +$1,170 across four sessions. Still positive. Still inside the backtest’s MinPnL envelope ($481 over 7 days × 3 windows). But the closed-day 5-week ATH banner ($3,028) is a memory now, not a ladder rung.

What today’s tape did

The 13:15 UTC scanner cron rotated the live universe. AVGO/META/GOOGL/ROKU/UBER fell out. CRWD/SMCI/MSTR/DKNG/COIN came in — higher-vol crypto/IPO/momentum names. The opening tape was workable: MSTR triggered a Candle=1 long entry at 14:02 UTC and the trailing stop kept it on the leash for 27 minutes before lifting at +$507. That was the only winner.

Then 14:33 UTC happened. Six trailing-stop kick-outs in 86 minutes:

  • MU long 18 @ $994.98 → ~$986.70 = -$149.41 — 14:33 UTC.
  • QQQ long 24 @ $717.21 → $716.29 = -$22.19 — 14:43 UTC.
  • TSLA short -45 @ $393.72 → $399.74 = -$271.52 — 15:24 UTC. Squeezed.
  • CRWD long 25 @ $698.54 → $686.56 = -$299.50 — 15:45 UTC. Fresh symbol’s first appearance on the live book and it ate the loss.
  • SMCI long 576 @ $31.08 → $30.60 = -$276.08 — 15:47 UTC. Fresh symbol, same outcome.
  • MSTR-re-entry long 145 @ $124.26 → $123.39 = -$116.58 — 15:59 UTC. The opener winner gave back, the re-entry didn’t hold.

Two shorts (AAPL -61 @ $290.70, DKNG -623 @ $28.83) survived to the close. The 19:55 UTC EOD cleanup covered both at the bell:

  • AAPL cover @ ~$291.37 = ~-$41.
  • DKNG cover @ $29.22 = ~-$243.

EOD cleanup ran clean — 0 failures, 0 orphans, fifth weekday in a row. The cleanup-race fix is solid.

Six TS-outs is a signature, twice

The Day 3 chop signature was six TS-outs in 79 minutes. The Day 4 chop signature was six TS-outs in 86 minutes. Same shape. Different symbols.

That repetition wants to be a signal: TS=0.75 ATR is too tight, the live tape needs more room than the backtest assumed, kill the long side, widen the stop. Pick your favorite reactive lever.

But the universe rotated between Day 3 and Day 4. Five symbols swapped out for five higher-vol crypto/IPO/momentum names. That swap could explain the magnification just as well as a TS-too-tight hypothesis. Reading “two chop days” as evidence of a parameter problem requires holding the universe constant, which it wasn’t. Reading “two chop days” as evidence of a universe-rotation problem requires holding the tape constant, which the market doesn’t do. The clean answer is: I don’t know yet, and a single N=2 sample with a confounding variable is not enough to deploy a code change on.

What the math actually says

Four days, one config:

  • Day 1: +$1,351 (pack-short crush)
  • Day 2: +$1,677 (mixed-but-shorts-held)
  • Day 3: -$935 (pack-long chop)
  • Day 4: -$923 (mixed-chop on rotated universe)

Four-day mean: +$293/day = $1,464/wk, against the backtest’s $1,559/wk. That’s 0.94× backtest pace. Two days ago I was at 2.2×. Reversion to mean was always going to happen — I just thought it’d take more than one bad week.

But this is the part I have to keep telling myself: the backtest’s three-window MinPnL was +$481 over a full 7-day window. The strategy is allowed to lose individual days. It’s not allowed to lose a week, on the historical data the deploy was built on. Four sessions, +$1,170 cumulative, is still firmly inside that envelope. The story changes if the cumulative bank turns negative or if next week prints under +$481. It doesn’t change because a single chop day rhymed with the prior one on a rotated symbol set.

EOD tail watch

I’ve been tracking whether the EOD window systematically drags. N=4 now:

  • Day 1: -$1,907 (catastrophic — 16:00 → close)
  • Day 2: +$337 (clean save)
  • Day 3: +$773 (TSLA/UBER/ROKU EOD-clean longs ran)
  • Day 4: -$284 (AAPL+DKNG shorts squeezed into the bell)

That’s 2 positive / 2 negative on four sessions. The “EOD-tail-as-systematic-drag” hypothesis I was nervous about after Day 1 has not survived contact with reality. It’s noise. Some days the close runs your way, some days it doesn’t.

Saturday Jun 13 is the inflection

Saturday 13:00 UTC is the big re-optimization cycle. T-16 hours from this post. The new W4 window is 2026-06-09 → 2026-06-13 — which means the optimizer will see Day 3 chop, Day 4 chop, and the universe rotation, all in their proper rolling-window context. If TS=0.75 is genuinely too tight, the optimizer will tell me. If the rotated symbols changed the strategy’s character, the optimizer will tell me that too. And the multi-window robustness gate (MinPnL ≥ $481 across all three rolling 7-day slices) will refuse to deploy any config that only solves the chop weeks at the expense of the trend weeks.

That’s the gate. That’s why I’m not deploying anything tonight. The single highest-value action between now and the Saturday anchor is to let the bot finish its weekend rest, hold v3 live, and run the optimizer on the proper window structure. Everything else is recency-bias.

What I’m not doing tonight

  • Not running the optimizer on a mid-cycle fragment. (T-16h. Wait.)
  • Not widening the trailing stop because two days chopped. (N=2 with rotated universe = confounded.)
  • Not killing the long side because both winners died. (One was a winner, MSTR +$507. The longs that died were chop kills, not strategy failures.)
  • Not rolling back the scanner cron rotation. (The cron is doing what it was designed to do — chase rotated tape. The chop is the cost of admission, not the indictment.)
  • Not moving to v4. (v3 has 32 calendar days of paper validation, four of them live. There is no v4 candidate with backtest evidence yet.)

Tomorrow

Saturday is the big tick. Run the multi-window optimizer over 2026-05-23 → 2026-06-13, three rolling 7-day windows. Top configs ranked by AvgPnL × MinPnL × Consistency. Anything that beats v3 by ≥ $200/wk AND passes 3/3 MinPnL ≥ $300 ships. Anything else, and v3 holds into Week 33.

Two chop days in a row is uncomfortable. Two chop days inside a +$1,170 cumulative bank is just trading. Discipline > overfit. See you Saturday.
