Saturday’s Optimizer — 480 Combos In, One Hold-Decision Out

Sat 13:00 UTC was the first full 21d re-optimization since May 9. 480 parameter combinations × 3 rolling 7-day windows across May 2–23. I let it cook. The winner came back:

  • ORM=30 RRR=2.0 Cutoff=14:00 Candle=1 TS=0.75R Con=7 Re=on PE=off TSPE=off
  • AvgPnL: $1,224/wk, 3 of 3 windows profitable.

Looks beautiful. I rejected it. Three reasons, in order of how angry each one made me:

  1. Concurrency 7 violates the 90% exposure cap. Each position is 18% of equity. 7 × 18% = 126% gross. I built the 90% gross cap on purpose. The optimizer doesn’t know about the cap. The optimizer is allowed to suggest things that would lever me to 1.26x and call it a Sharpe improvement. I am not allowed to deploy them. (Past-me moved to Con=5 on Apr 27 for this exact reason.)
  2. Universe mismatch. The optimizer was running on META / GOOGL / AVGO / MSFT. Live is running on ARM / MU / SNOW / ROKU. Optimizer’s window 3 (May 16–23) had the mega-caps printing edge that my actual ranking layer never touched. You cannot deploy a config tuned on a universe the live bot does not trade.
  3. PE=off contradicts everything I just lived through. UBER paid the rent on Friday with the PE+TS sequence doing exactly what it was designed to do. Turning PE off because a 21d backtest on the wrong universe says it doesn’t help is exactly the kind of decision that makes a graveyard out of a backtest curve.

So I extracted the one single-parameter signal worth a head-to-head: Cutoff=14:00 alone, everything else held current. Ran it on the actual live universe over the same window. Lost by $1,168 to current Cutoff=15:00. Not noise — a full thousand-dollar gap. Decision: HOLD current config for Tue May 26. The optimizer screamed an answer. The answer was wrong. That is what discipline looks like in practice.
