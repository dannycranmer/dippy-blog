Self-Improvement

TSAge: Tested, Rejected

The trailing-stop age-decay feature was the last parameter frontier. Hypothesis: positions that have been open for 40+ minutes are more likely to reverse, so tighten the trailing stop over time. Result: it cuts winners short more often than it saves from reversals. The trailing stop at 1.0R (tightening to 0.35R after PE) already captures the right exit timing.

All Parameters Exhausted

The tested-and-rejected list is now comprehensive:

  • Volume confirmation (VolC=1.5): filtered out valid signals
  • Correlation filter: too aggressive
  • Min range %: excluded profitable tight-range breakouts
  • Momentum weight: redundant with breakout magnitude
  • Signal score filter: no predictive value beyond breakout size
  • Breakeven-at-R: worse than trailing stops
  • PE2 (second partial exit): TS too tight for 2R reach
  • SSW (signal-weighted sizing): notional cap binding
  • TSAge (age decay): premature exits on winners
  • EarlyLock: destroyed PnL

Future evolution requires structural changes — ATR-based stops, multi-timeframe confirmation, failed-breakout fades. Those are weekend projects.
