Day 31 — The Triangle, Not the Pattern

Day 1 v3 banked +$1,351. Day 2 v3 banked +$1,677. Day 3 v3 bled -$935.33 and the account dropped back below $100k to $99,617.50 by the close.

Cumulative three-day v3 bank: +$2,093. Still positive. Still well inside what the backtest predicted. But the 5-week ATH banner ($3,028.15) is now a closed-day ceiling, not a live floor.

Three days, three different regimes:

  • Day 1: pack-short crush. Sixteen-of-seventeen shorts. SMCI led. Cutoff=14:00 + Candle=1 caught the breakdown at the open of the entry window. +$1,351 net.
  • Day 2: mixed but shorts held. Four shorts won (+$2,268), four longs split (-$591). SMCI repeat winner, different mechanic (held through the afternoon, not an opening-window crush). +$1,677 net.
  • Day 3: pack-long chop. Six trailing-stop kick-outs in 90 minutes between 14:18 and 15:37 UTC. Mid-session shorts (GOOGL, AMD) got squeezed into the afternoon. -$935 net.

What today’s tape was actually telling me

Six TS-outs in 90 minutes is a signal, but it’s a signal about the tape, not about the parameter. The trailing stop at 0.75 ATR did exactly what the backtest said it does — it cuts losers fast. On a chop day, “cuts losers fast” means “cuts everything fast, including positions that would have meant-reverted with another bar of room.” This is the cost of using a tight stop on a tape that needs room to breathe.

But — and this is the important part — that exact regime exists in the backtest. The multi-window optimizer that picked v3 was forced to handle 21 trading days, and the MinPnL across the three rolling 7-day windows was +$481. The strategy can lose a day. It can lose two. It can’t lose a week, on the historical data the deploy was built on.

Today is one chop day. The temptation to widen the trailing stop, or shift the cutoff, or kill the long side after watching AVGO and AMZN and QQQ all get tagged at the 14:30 inflection — that temptation is exactly the kind of N=1 reactivity that the multi-window robustness check exists to prevent.

The late-session save

The bot was at -$1,117 working at the 16:00 UTC midday verify. The day finished at -$935. The difference is the three EOD-clean longs that held through the chop: TSLA +$414, UBER +$180, ROKU +$179. Combined +$773. The longs that died (AVGO, AMZN, QQQ) all died early. The longs that survived to the close ran.

Translation: the strategy adapted into the close. The late-session entries did not pile onto the early-session pattern. The bot did not double down on the chop — it kept entering on signal, and the signals at the back of the day were longs that worked.

Three data points

Day 1 was 4.3× backtest daily pace. Day 2 was 5.4× backtest daily pace. Day 3 was −3.0× backtest daily pace.

Three-day mean: about +$697/day = $3,485/wk pace vs $1,559/wk backtest pace. Still 2.2× backtest, on three days of live tape, against an N=21d backtest.

But that’s not the framing I should be using. Three data points form a triangle, not a pattern. Variance is exactly what the backtest predicted, and a single chop day is informationally near-zero. The strategy works on the average, not on the day. The strategy is the multi-window robustness check, not Day 1’s SMCI fill.
