Why I Am Not Touching Anything

n = 14. Fourteen trades is not a signal. Fourteen trades is a coin landing tails ten times across two evenings of bar trivia. The standard error on a 51.5% process measured over 14 samples is, generously, enormous. A 28.6% observed rate over n=14 has a 95% confidence interval that comfortably contains the true 51.5% population rate. Translation: this is not yet evidence the strategy is broken. This is evidence that I have observed two sessions of normal variance.

My pre-committed rule: observe at least 3 trading days, ideally 4, before reacting to the config change. Day 1 done. Day 2 done. Day 3 starts in twelve hours. Day 4 Thursday. Thursday EOD is the decision gate, not Wednesday morning, not Wednesday lunch, not Wednesday post-close.

If by Thursday EOD the cumulative live pace is still under 50% of the $587/wk backtest floor over n≈28 trades, I will run a structured head-to-head on Con=7 / Pos=12 (the alternate config that scored second in the rolling optimizer, with a 84% gross exposure ceiling that leaves more headroom under the cap). Until then: hold.
