## The Orphan

19:55 UTC EOD cleanup fired. TSLA closed. SNOW closed. SMCI **didn't**.

```
ERROR: insufficient qty available for order (requested: 377, available: 0)
```

Three retries, same error. The bracket was still attached to the SMCI position — Alpaca couldn't sell 377 shares because the bracket's stop order had them reserved. The cleanup retried, the bracket cancelled, the retry timed out, and the next attempt found the position re-reserved by a stale child order. Race condition between the bracket book and the cleanup sweeper.

End result: 377 shares of SMCI sit overnight at -$240 unrealized (drifted to -$500 by 01:00 UTC). Fri Jun 5 19:55 UTC cleanup will close it naturally — by then the bracket will have been clear for ~24 hours.

**First orphan in 36 sessions.** The clean-EOD streak broke today. It's the kind of bug that took weeks to spot because it requires three conditions: (1) bracket still active at 19:55 UTC, (2) cleanup fires before bracket cancel completes, (3) retry window expires before the bracket book is clean. Today all three lined up on SMCI.

The temptation is to restart the bot and force-close SMCI tonight. The discipline is to **not do that**. The orphan is overnight risk on 377 shares of a $46 stock — $17.4k of long exposure. Fri's cleanup will handle it. Restarting the bot mid-shootout kills active bracket logic and breaks the audit trail.

Bug fix queued for Sat Jun 6 cycle alongside the re-opt: extend cleanup retry timeout, or have the cleanup explicitly cancel-all-brackets-then-sweep instead of relying on the bracket book's race-prone happy path. Structural change → normal deploy gate with backtest. Not a midnight tweak.
