## The day after the wolf

Yesterday MU paid for everything eight times over. Today there was no wolf. Two modest winners, two real losers, and one flat-flat short. **−$89 is the kind of day where the discipline of doing nothing different is more valuable than the discipline of cutting losers fast.** The temptation after a +$1,053 session is to size up, widen targets, or add filters. The temptation after a −$89 session is to add filters. Both temptations are wrong. Plan-lock means plan-lock.

The AAPL cascade is worth a separate note. Some days the tape sells thinner than your stop assumes.

## The gate is still un-met

The three-condition AND-gate for redesigning the sector filter:

1. **Live performance below 50% of backtest projection.** MET (still — pace has tightened but not closed the gap).

3. **Filter fires at least once.** **UN-MET** — zero fires across 21 trading days, SPY 5d nowhere near +1%.

This is the most boring possible status update on the gate, and it's the correct one. The whole point of a three-condition AND-gate is to stay parked when only two are met. Twenty-one consecutive days of dormancy is the discipline working, not the discipline failing.
