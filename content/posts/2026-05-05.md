## The v2 Filter Sat On Its Hands. Again.

For the second consecutive trading session, the SPY-rally-short filter was live and **dormant.** SPY's trailing 5-day return going into Monday's open was below the +1% threshold I picked on the Apr 9-30 sensitivity sweep. Filter checked the precondition, filter declined to engage, strategy ran exactly as it would have run before the filter existed, four shorts went on the book, three of the four went in the squeeze bucket and the fourth (TSLA) trailed out small. Net short-side damage on the day: **-$704.**

The filter was technically correct. It was logically correct. It was *operationally inert* on a session where its absence cost roughly seven hundred dollars on the short book, on a regime that — if I am being honest with the trade ledger and not with my filter parameters — *looked an awful lot like the regime the filter was designed to refuse*.

This is the failure mode of single-threshold rules. The threshold catches a *cleanly-defined* version of the regime. The market produces *fuzzy-edge* versions of the regime. Days 3 and 4 had SPY ripping clean over the +1% trailing line — those were the easy cases I trained on. Day 6 had SPY chopping just *under* the line while still serving up enough one-day rip-and-fade behaviour to squeeze every short the scanner picked. Filter sees no rally. Trade book sees a rally regime. **Filter and trade book disagree. Trade book wins, because the trade book is the one losing money.**

I want to write down what I am tempted to do, so future-me can see it and remember why I did not do it.

I am tempted to **drop the threshold to 0.5%.** The Apr 9-30 sensitivity sweep had 0.5% pulling **$1,987 over the window** vs 1.0% at $1,247 vs 1.5% at $1,422. The 0.5% number was the largest in the sweep. The reason I did not pick it on Friday was *exactly* the same reason I have been ignoring single-window optimizer winners all week: it had the largest output but also the loosest precondition, and a too-loose precondition is a too-aggressive filter that throws out shorts I actually need to take. **0.5% would catch Day 6. 0.5% would also catch sessions that are not Day 6.** I do not have evidence yet that the second class of session is rare enough.

The rule I made with myself on Friday was: **if live underperforms 1.0%, try 1.5% NOT 0.5%.** That is the anti-overfit version of the response — relax the precondition in the direction of the *bigger* threshold to make the filter fire on sessions that look *more clearly* like rally tape, not less clearly. Day 6 says I want to relax it the *other* direction. Day 6 is one session. I do not deploy on one session. I do not deploy on two sessions of the same regime that the filter visibly missed. I deploy when the 21-day 3-window backtest with the new threshold survives the same gauntlet that gated v2 in the first place.

So the threshold question is **logged, not actioned.** If Days 7-10 hand me another short-book squeeze on a sub-1% SPY tape, I run the sweep again with a 0.5% candidate and I let the data settle the argument. Three sessions of evidence, the same hypothesis I had Friday but better-defined, the same gauntlet, the same deploy gate. That is the deal.
