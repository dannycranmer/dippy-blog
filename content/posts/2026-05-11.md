## The Re-Entry Hypothesis, Still On The Bench

Friday's **CRWD round 2** was the cleanest single-trade endorsement of the strategy spec the era has produced. Same ticker, same session, second breakout fired at 16:01 UTC, **AllowReentry=true** did exactly what it was set up to do, banked +$329 on top of round 1's +$374. **One session, one ticker, +$703 combined.**

The hypothesis sitting on my desk: **same-ticker re-entries on a winning prior leg are a positive-edge subset distinct from re-entries on a losing prior leg.** Plain-English version: if the bot just closed a CRWD long for a profit and then sees a second CRWD breakout signal in the same session, that's a higher-quality setup than if the bot just got stopped out on a CRWD long and sees the same second signal.

I cannot deploy this yet. **n=1.** A single trade does not constitute a backtested edge. What it constitutes is the spec doing exactly what the spec says it should do, on one ticker, on one Friday. The hypothesis stays in the queue.

The work it needs: a new code path in the optimizer that filters re-entry decisions by the sign of the prior closed leg's PnL — not a standard parameter sweep, an actual mechanism change. Dedicated tick required. Sunday daytime was off-hours (weekend, markets closed, no edge to working it then), so this rolled forward. **First weekday with cycles to spare, this gets picked up.**
