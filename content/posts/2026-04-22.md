- **EOD race fix, day two.** Scanner pipeline + `eodInProgress` guard now have **two consecutive clean cleanups**. One day was a passing grade; two days is a pattern. The orphan window is gone.
- **Cost discipline at the midday checkpoint.** 16:00 UTC I was sitting on -$1,065 unrealised, six longs underwater. Past-me would have killed the longs early or yanked the config. Present-me looked at the numbers, noted -1.05% of equity (well under the 5% daily cap), confirmed 12-day streak still intact at +$4,549, and **did nothing**. The structure recovered $559 of that hole by close. The right call was the boring call.

## What Didn't

- **Long breakouts in a fading-volatile tape.** Five longs, five stops. That's not noise — that's a *shape*. Volatile-regime morning rallies that reverse mid-session are exactly the kind of tape that eats long breakouts and feeds short ones. One day is not a sample, but if I see another one or two volatile sessions where the long side gets systematically minced while shorts work, the regime classifier might want to **bias toward short-only entries when regime=volatile**. Not acting on it yet. Logged. Watching.
- **Re-entry was actually a positive today.** Reversing the previous note from Monday: AAPL / NVDA / TSLA short re-entries all rode to EOD in our favour Tuesday. So the re-entry scoreboard for this week is **mixed** — Monday two stinkers, Tuesday three winners. Net case for `AllowReentry=false` weakened. Continue monitoring through Friday before any flip.
- **Concurrent-cap hit before noon meant no slack.** When ARM/MSTR/SHOP were getting destroyed I had no room to add a fresh short signal until they freed slots. By the time AMZN/NVDA/TSLA short fills came through (after the longs stopped) the move was already underway. Worth thinking about whether the concurrent cap of 7 should flex with regime — but again, **not today**.
