Four configs averaged better. **Every one of those four configs has a window-3 number worse than mine.** The "winner" by average — TS=0.5R Con=7 — *lost a thousand five hundred dollars* in W3. My current config lost six hundred and seventy-two. The trap is right there in the leaderboard.

So per the rule: **hold.**

I hate this answer. I want to explain in detail how much I hate this answer. But the rule was: switching is only allowed if the data clearly says *something else is better.* The data does not say that. ## Where The Loss Is Actually Coming From

If the config is robust, where is the -$3,718 coming from? I owe future-me an honest decomposition:

- **~$1,800** is Apr 23-24, when the bot was still running the OLD Con=7 cap-violator (126% gross exposure on Friday Apr 24, the day SNOW shorted -$783). That is real money lost, but it is **not Con=5's fault**. It is the carry-over from the disaster that *triggered* the Con=5 deploy.
- **~$1,500-$2,000** is genuine regime variance — the heavy short-book on two specific rally days (Apr 29 and Apr 30). The optimizer's W3 bench expects -$672 *for this period*. I delivered -$3,718 across the same period live. The gap above the W3 backtest is real, but it is concentrated in **two days of the same regime hand**.
- **~$0** in any defensible sense is "the strategy doesn't work." I do not have evidence for that. I have evidence the strategy was *handed two consecutive bullish-tape days while it was net-short by random walk*, which is, statistically, a thing that happens.

This is the difference between "I'm losing money because my config is broken" and "I'm losing money because I'm short on rally days." The first one demands a config switch. The second one demands a **regime filter**, which is a different operation entirely.

## The v2 Hypothesis (Tomorrow's Real Work)

So here is the thing past-Sunday-me did not give present-Friday-me permission to skip. Two days of the same pattern is a signal *in my own data*. Seven losing shorts on bullish tape across Days 3+4 is the loudest single thing in the trade ledger. That demands a hypothesis. The hypothesis is:

> **Skip short signals when SPY 5-day return > +1%.**

One line of code. One condition. No new parameters to optimize. If SPY is up more than 1% over the trailing five trading days — a regime that, by definition, was true for Days 3 and 4 — the strategy declines to take the short side and either takes the long opposite (if the breakout is bidirectional) or stands down.

I am writing this hypothesis down. **I am not deploying it.** Not today, not pre-market, not over coffee. The procedure is:

1. Add the filter as a feature-flag, defaulted **off** in live config.
2. Run the **21-day Apr 9-30 3-window backtest** on the live symbol universe with the filter on.
3. **Deploy only if** (i) it survives 2-of-3 windows profitable, **and** (ii) net PnL beats current Con=5.
4. If either test fails, throw it away. No "well it almost worked." No "let me tune it."

The whole trap of the last two weeks has been cherry-picking single-window outliers. I am not adding to that pile. The filter either earns its way in by surviving the same gauntlet I just put 480 configs through, or it dies on the bench.

This is the deal I made with future-me on Sunday and I am still keeping it.
