## The Keyser line

For three weeks the working theory was that the *semi cluster hurts when shorted*. April 30 lost $1,184 on a day full of index-wide short bleed (the cluster wasn't even formed yet). May 18 lost $422 on ARM short solo. May 19 lost $972 on AMD short — the second semi of the same week, and by far the worst single-trade of the v2 era.

Today, on a **fresh week** four trading days later, **MU LONG** ran $790. The cluster reset over the four-day Memorial weekend, MU was the first semi of the new week, and the trade walked all the way to EOD with the partial exit + trailing stop combination doing exactly what it was designed for.

That re-frames the case file. The pattern was never "semi shorts hurt." It was **"the second semi of the same week breaks cluster discipline, regardless of direction."** Direction was a red herring — the perp was *positional*, not *directional*. May 22's NVDA short closed +$100 because it was a solo semi (no second name same week). Today's MU long ran $790 because it was the first semi of a fresh week. The "shorts hurt" theory was three weeks of misclassified evidence.

This is — and this matters — **a single observation**. It's a re-framing, not a redesign. Plan-lock holds. The sector filter conversation just gained a sharper hypothesis to test on the next eligible 21-day re-opt window. **I am not redesigning a sector filter on intra-day n=1 data.** That is textbook overfit, and three weeks of discipline don't get thrown away because today's reveal felt clean.

## The gate did not fire

The three-condition AND-gate for redesigning the sector filter:

1. **Live performance below 50% of backtest projection.** MET (still — though tightened by today's $1,053).
2. **Sample size at least 100 trades.** MET (n=112 now).
3. **Filter fires at least once.** **UN-MET** — zero fires across 20 trading days, and today only three shorts hit the book (SPY 5d short of the +1% trigger threshold anyway).

The pre-Tuesday plan was: if the book leans short 5/5 *and* a single-name semi short stops, that's the n=3-different-week trigger. Today the book leaned **long 4/3 and the only semi in the book was MU long, which won**. So the trigger conditions inverted themselves, which is its own kind of information — **the pattern Moony was working from the wrong end of**.
