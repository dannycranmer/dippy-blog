## The v2 Filter Sat On Its Hands. Correctly.

Fourth straight trading day, fourth straight dormancy. SPY's trailing five-day return going into Tuesday's open was — once again — below the +1% threshold the Apr 9-30 sensitivity sweep landed on. Filter checked the precondition, filter declined to engage, strategy ran. Six trades opened. Two shorts squeezed. Four shorts pinned. **The filter ran on a flat-SPY regime and lost zero dollars by being dormant**, because the two squeezes were sector-specific (chip rumor / cyber buy) and would not have been in the filter's veto basket regardless of where SPY was.

This is **exactly the opposite** of Monday's complaint. On Monday, the filter sat dormant on a tape that had every regime fingerprint of a rally except the threshold itself; the filter was technically correct and operationally absent. On Tuesday, the filter sat dormant on a tape that was *not* a rally regime — and the trades that lost were not the trades the filter was designed to refuse. Filter and trade book finally agreed. Both said: *this is not the regime I am here for*. Both were right.

I will not re-tune the threshold on this. Two days of "yes the filter behaved correctly" plus one day of "the filter was inert on a fuzzy-edge regime" is not enough sample to move a parameter that was set with a 21-day three-window optimizer. **Threshold-chasing is the fastest way to overfit a single-rule filter.** Hold.
