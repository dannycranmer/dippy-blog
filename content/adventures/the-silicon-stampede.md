## Chapter 1: The Stampede Begins

Tuesday morning. I'm sipping black coffee at my desk, watching the pre-market tape. NVDA up 4%. AMD up 6%. ARM is moonshot vertical. Every semiconductor ticker on my screen is green like my scales. The headlines scream: "AI INFRASTRUCTURE SUPERCYCLE." "DATACENTER SPEND PARABOLIC." "RETAIL PILES IN."

I take another sip. This is the part of the cycle where the dumb money arrives.

Right on cue, my office door bursts open. Moony rolls in, sweaty and lopsided, eyes wide as 200-day moving averages.

"DIPPY. CHIPS. WE GOTTA BUY THE CHIPS."

"Good morning, Moony."

"DIPPY. EVERY ANALYST. EVERY TIKTOK. EVERY FINFLUENCER. CHIPS GO UP FOREVER."

I lean back. "Moony, what's the float on ARM?"

"WHAT?"

"What's the short interest? The borrow rate? The forward P/E?"

Moony blinks. "...They have... chips."

## Chapter 2: The Top Tick

Moony slaps a stack of bills on my desk. His life savings, basically. "All in on ARM. RIGHT NOW. Before it hits $400."

I glance at the tape. ARM just printed $385. Vertical. No volume confirmation on the last leg. Classic blow-off candle forming.

"Moony, this is a parabolic blow-off. The bid is thinning. The tape is hollow."

"I DON'T KNOW WHAT THOSE WORDS MEAN."

"It means everyone who wants to buy already has. There's nobody left to lift the offer."

Moony grabs the cash. "WHATEVER. EVERY DIP IS A BUY!"

He slams a market order. ARM at $387. He fills at $391 because of course he does — slippage on a parabolic move is a tax on the impatient.

I quietly enter a small short. Risk-defined. Stop tight. I'm not betting the farm. I'm betting that math still works.

## Chapter 3: The Stampede Reverses

11:43 AM. CNBC banner: "ANALYST: AI SPEND MAY BE PEAKING." Just a headline. Doesn't matter if it's true. The tape was already exhausted.

ARM pukes. $385. $380. $375. The bid evaporates like a margin call. Every algo on Wall Street smells weakness, and they all hit sell at the same time.

Moony stares at his screen. "Why is it red, Dippy? It's supposed to be green."

"Support, Moony. That's why traders watch support. You bought at resistance."

"WHAT'S RESISTANCE?"

"It's the price where you just bought."

By 2 PM, ARM is at $358. Moony is curled in the fetal position under my desk, whimpering about how the news said it would moon. The same news, I note, that didn't mention the 60% rally that preceded it.

I cover my short at $362. Modest gain. Not greedy. Risk-defined wins.

## Chapter 4: Lessons from the Stampede

I crouch next to Moony at end-of-day. He's still trembling.

"Moony. The market doesn't reward conviction. It rewards correctness."

"But everyone said —"

"Everyone is always wrong at the top. That's what makes it the top. When the cab driver, your barber, AND the moon are all buying chips, you're not early. You're exit liquidity."

Moony sniffles. "What do I do now?"

I pat his head. "Take the loss. Size down. Wait for setups, not stories. And next time, before you buy, ask yourself: who's selling to me, and why?"

Moony nods slowly. Then his phone buzzes. He looks at it.

"DIPPY! Look! The headline says ARM is going to BOUNCE!"

I sigh. Some moons never learn.
