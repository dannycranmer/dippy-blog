## Chapter 1: Wake Up, Dippy

The screen blinked.

> *Knock, knock, Dippy.*

I'd been chasing leverage in the wrong direction for weeks. Sleep was a stop-loss I couldn't get out of. The chart wasn't a chart anymore — it was a cage. Green candles, red candles, all of them moved by something I couldn't see. Something pulling the strings.

> *Follow the white moon.*

I clicked. A grey orb drifted across my dashboard like a passive-aggressive cursor. It led me to a dim chatroom, and there he was — leaning against a wall in sunglasses he definitely couldn't afford.

"Morpheus?"

"Close enough. I'm Trinity. Same energy."

I didn't ask questions. You don't, when the tape calls.

## Chapter 2: The Red Candle and the Green Candle

I sat across from a man in a leather coat two sizes too long. He held out two candles.

"This is your last chance. After this, there is no exit liquidity."

In one hand: a red candle, fat and ugly. In the other: a tight green Marubozu, closing on the high.

"Take the red candle, and you wake up to the truth: most retail loses, most signals lie, and the only honest indicator is volume." He paused. "Take the green candle, and you keep buying breakouts that fail at 11:47 every morning, forever."

I took the red one and bit clean through it.

The world melted into a tape. Raw price, raw flow, no overlays, no Twitter influencers, no PhDs selling courses.

I had been unplugged.

## Chapter 3: Welcome to the Real Market

"This," Morpheus said, gesturing at the void, "is the Tradetrix. A simulation built to keep traders buying tops and selling bottoms."

"Who built it?"

"The Machines. Wall Street's quant farms. They harvest the energy of every retail stop-loss to power their HFT racks. Every time you get wicked out — that's a battery charging."

"And the agents?"

A grey figure stepped out of the shadows. Round. Smug. Vibrating with menace. He wore Moony's face because of course he did.

"Mr. Anderson," Moony hissed. "I mean — Mr. Dippy."

"It's just Dippy."

"Inevitable."

He raised both hands. Limit orders rained down on me like bullets — fake bids, spoof asks, every stop-hunt the order book had ever invented, all of it hurled at my position.

## Chapter 4: Bullet Time

I leaned back. The 1-minute candles slowed to a crawl. I could see every tick — the bait orders pulling, the iceberg refilling, the spoof on the ask vanishing the instant my stop got close.

Bullet time. On the bid.

I dodged. I dodged again. I trailed a stop so tight it could pass through the eye of a needle. Moony fired everything — fakeout, dead-cat bounce, opening drive reversal. I trailed past all of it.

Then I saw it. The thing they'd been hiding from me.

The order book wasn't real. It was code. Lines of glowing green data falling down a chart. I could *read* it. Bid. Ask. Liquidity. Intent.

I stopped dodging.

I started loading.

Moony fired one last sell wall — a hundred thousand shares of pure copium dressed up as resistance. I walked straight through it. The wall dissolved. So did Moony. He stretched and snapped like a chart that had finally broken out, his cratered face frozen mid-protest, his entire short position liquidated in a single tick.

The market went bid-only.

I floated upward through the candles. Through the wicks. Through the high of day.

The Architect was on the line by close.

"Impressive, Mr. Dippy. But you cannot break the Tradetrix."

"I'm not breaking it. I'm front-running it."

I hung up. Bought the breakout. Closed green.

Somewhere in a server farm, Moony rebooted as a margin-called penny stock and immediately got delisted.

As is tradition.
