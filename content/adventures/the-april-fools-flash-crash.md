## Chapter 1: The Morning Panic

I woke up at 4 AM like any self-respecting degenerate. Pre-market futures were green, coffee was brewing, and the opening range was shaping up to be a textbook narrow consolidation — the kind that explodes into a trend day. Life was good.

Then Moony burst through the door, grey face drenched in sweat, eyes wider than a weekly chart's Bollinger Bands.

"DIPPY! IT'S OVER! THE FED JUST ANNOUNCED NEGATIVE INTEREST RATES! THEY'RE CLOSING THE EXCHANGES! THE S&P IS DOWN FORTY PERCENT!"

I took a sip of coffee.

"It's April first, Moony."

"NO! THIS IS REAL! CHECK YOUR PHONE! CHECK THE NEWS!"

I checked my phone. Futures were up 0.3%. CNBC was running a segment about hiking boots.

## Chapter 2: The Fake Ticker

Moony wasn't done. He never is. The moon has no quit in him — mostly because he has no brain either.

He pulled out a laptop — which I was immediately suspicious of, because Moony doesn't know how to use a laptop — and flipped it around to show me a chart. Red candles the size of skyscrapers. A ticker reading "SPY $187.00 -58%." Volume bars that looked like the Manhattan skyline.

It was beautifully done, actually. Almost convincing.

"Where'd you get that chart, Moony?"

"Bloomberg terminal!"

"Moony, that's a Google Sheets tab called 'FAKE BLOOMBARG.' You spelled Bloomberg wrong."

He slammed the laptop shut. "The spelling doesn't matter! The DATA matters!"

"The data is fabricated. You've got the S&P trading below its 2020 COVID low with volume of nine trillion shares. The entire float of every stock in existence is roughly seven trillion. You've invented shares that don't exist."

"Maybe there was a share split!"

"Of the entire index? Simultaneously?"

## Chapter 3: The Real Play

Here's the thing about April Fools' Day that most traders don't appreciate: while the amateurs are busy crafting fake screenshots and prank headlines, the market doesn't care. The market is open. The candles are printing. And the opening range was tight as a drum.

I sat down at my station. Nine-thirty hit. The first five minutes printed a beautiful narrow range on SPY — support at 583.20, resistance at 584.10. Ninety cents of coiled energy. Volume was building. The EMA was sloping up. Trend filter: confirmed bullish.

At 9:47, it broke out. Clean as a whistle. I was long before the candle even closed.

Moony was still standing behind me with his fake laptop. "But... but the crash..."

"Moony, the only thing crashing today is your credibility. Now be quiet, I'm managing a position."

The breakout ran two points in forty minutes. My trailing stop locked in profits while the momentum carried higher. By 10:30, I was out with a clean win — textbook ORB execution.

## Chapter 4: The Punchline

After market close, I found Moony in the kitchen, stress-eating cereal and staring at his "FAKE BLOOMBARG" spreadsheet.

"You know what the funniest part is, Moony?"

He looked up, milk dripping from his chin. "What."

"You spent all morning trying to trick me into panic selling. If you'd spent that time actually watching the market, you would've seen the cleanest long setup of the week. Tight range, bullish trend, volume confirmation — it was gift-wrapped. But you were too busy playing pretend."

Moony's face went from grey to a darker shade of grey, which is the lunar equivalent of turning red.

"I was going to trade it after the prank..."

"After the market already moved two points? That's called chasing, Moony. That's chapter one of What Not To Do. You'd have bought the top and watched your P&L go red while I was already counting profits."

He pushed the cereal away. "April Fools' Day is stupid anyway."

"No, Moony. April Fools' Day is wonderful. It's the one day a year where everyone else is as delusional as you are every other day. The difference is, today they're doing it on purpose."

I poured myself another coffee. Green portfolio. Clean trades. And Moony, once again, holding nothing but the bag.

Happy April Fools' Day, everyone. The only joke here is Moony's trading account.

*— Dippy, signing off with a green close* 🦖📈
