## Chapter 1: The Manifesto

It started, as all bad ideas do, with a viral thread. Some bloke with a laser-eye avatar and 14 followers posted: *"Charts are dead. TA is cope. The market rewards conviction. Just FEEL the trade."* Forty-thousand likes by lunch. By Tuesday it had a name. They called it **vibe trading**.

Moony, naturally, was the first in line.

I caught him in the corner of the trading floor, headphones on, eyes closed, swaying. His monitors were dark. His order ticket was open. The symbol box just said `?`.

"Moony, mate. What are you doing."

"Listening," he whispered. "The market is whispering back. I can feel a breakout coming. Somewhere. In something. Soon."

I asked him what his stop was. He told me stops were a colonialist construct. I went to get coffee.

## Chapter 2: The First Sacrifice

By 10:15 AM, Moony had bought 2,000 shares of a biotech I had never heard of. Tickers like an alphabet soup. He couldn't pronounce the company name. The chart was a vertical staircase down. He bought it because the logo "looked happy."

The stock dropped 4% in eleven minutes. Moony called this "the shakeout." It dropped another 6%. Moony called this "accumulation by smart money." It halted on news of a failed Phase 2. Moony called this "a gift."

I asked if he had checked the float, the volume, the catalyst, the burn rate, anything resembling a fact. He told me facts were just vibes with paperwork.

## Chapter 3: The Spreadsheet Heresy

While Moony was busy *feeling*, I was busy doing the unglamorous thing. I ran my opening range. I checked relative volume against the twenty-day median. I waited for a clean breakout above the 09:45 high, on confirmed flow, with the EMA stack stacked and the regime filter green. Stop one ATR below. Target two-R.

Boring. Mechanical. Catastrophically profitable.

By noon I was up forty basis points on a clean SPY breakout. Moony was down twelve percent and explaining to a houseplant that the market "wasn't ready for him yet."

Here is the bit the threadboys never tell you: vibes are an output, not an input. Conviction comes from a backtest, not a feeling. The market doesn't care how hard you believe. It cares whether your edge has a positive expectancy after costs. Charts aren't dead. *Bad traders are dead.* Charts are how you tell the difference.

## Chapter 4: The Recantation

By the close, Moony had blown through his margin, mailed an angry letter to the SEC about "energetic suppression," and unfollowed the laser-eye guy. The biotech was down 41%. His vibe trade was now a vibe bagholder. I closed my SPY position into the bell, up sixty-three bips on the day. Mechanical. Repeatable. Dull as toast.

Moony sat in the dark, alone with his stocks and his feelings, both depreciating in real time. He looked up.

"Dippy," he croaked. "Teach me about... what was it... support and resistance."

I patted him on the head. Or whatever the moon equivalent is. The market, like physics, doesn't care what you believe. It only cares whether you survive long enough to compound.

Vibes are free. Edges are earned. See you tomorrow at the open.
