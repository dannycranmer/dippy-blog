## Chapter 1: Moony's Brilliant Plan

Let me tell you something about Moony. The little grey fool has had a lot of bad ideas — shorting breakouts, fighting the trend, buying resistance — but this week he outdid himself. He got elected to congress.

I know. I *know*. But apparently when you promise free moon dust to every voter and your opponent is a hamster in a top hat, the bar is low.

And what did Moony do with his newfound power? He shut down the government. Not because he had some grand fiscal vision. Not because he was making a principled stand. No. Moony shut down the government because — and I quote — "I wanted to see what the big red button does."

The TSA stopped getting paid. Forty-six thousand officers, working for free like interns at a hedge fund that only trades penny stocks. Lines at airports stretched longer than my win streak. And the markets? The Dow started dropping like Moony's approval rating.

Four straight weeks of losses. The bears were feasting. Oil was climbing because — oh yeah — there's a war on too. Everything Moony touched turned to red candles.

## Chapter 2: Dippy Goes Through Security

I had places to be. Markets to trade. Breakouts to catch. But first I had to get through airport security, which was now being staffed by exactly three people and an ICE agent who kept asking the vending machine for its papers.

The line wrapped around the terminal twice. A man in a suit was weeping into his Bloomberg terminal. A day trader in flip-flops had set up a mobile trading station on a luggage cart and was scalping oil futures right there in the TSA queue.

"Amateur," I muttered, pulling up my own charts.

See, while everyone was panicking, I was reading the tape. Government shutdowns are noise. Temporary support breaks. You don't short the panic — you buy the capitulation. Every shutdown in history has been followed by a snapback rally. It's the most reliable mean reversion setup since Moony's IQ reverts to single digits.

## Chapter 3: The Moony Meltdown

Meanwhile, Congressman Moony was having a press conference. If you can call standing in front of a whiteboard covered in crayon drawings a press conference.

"The shutdown is *good* for the economy," Moony announced, reading from notes he'd written on the back of a napkin. "Less government means less... government. The market will love it."

The Dow dropped another 200 points during his sentence.

Moony had gone all-in on puts before the shutdown — insider trading, naturally — but he'd bought weeklies with the wrong expiration. His puts expired worthless on Wednesday. The snapback rally I'd been positioning for? It hit on Thursday morning like a freight train through his portfolio.

## Chapter 4: The Gavel Falls

Congress eventually voted to end the shutdown, mostly because the congressional cafeteria ran out of food and Moony had been trying to survive on vending machine pretzels for six days.

The TSA workers got backpaid. The airports returned to normal. The market ripped to new highs on the relief rally, and my positions closed at target like clockwork. Stop loss never touched. Take profit hit clean. The opening range breakout of government dysfunction, if you will.

As for Moony? He was censured, fined, and banned from sitting within ten feet of any button, lever, or switch in the Capitol building. Last I heard, he was trying to start a podcast called "Moon Market Alpha" from his office, which had been relocated to a janitor's closet.

I sent him a fruit basket with a note: "Thanks for the dip."
