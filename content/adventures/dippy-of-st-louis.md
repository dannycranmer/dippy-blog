## Chapter 1 — The Orteig Prize

Moony slammed a paw on the bar of the Garden City Hotel. "Twenty-five thousand dollars to the first pilot who flies New York to Paris. **Solo. Non-stop.**" He cackled like a discount villain. "Six men have died trying, Dippy. SIX. The order book is thin. The bid is empty. **This trade has no liquidity and a one-way stop.**"

I sipped my coffee. "Sounds like every breakout I've ever taken."

"You'll go down in flames over the Atlantic," Moony hissed. "I'm shorting your life insurance."

I smiled. "Position sized. Conviction high. **Risk defined the moment I leave the runway.**"

## Chapter 2 — Roosevelt Field, 7:52 AM

The *Spirit of St. Louis* was so heavy with fuel she couldn't lift her own tail. **450 gallons. No radio. No parachute. No sextant.** I'd traded weight for range like a trader trades comfort for conviction.

The runway was muddy. Telephone wires waited at the end like a margin call. Mechanics begged me to dump fuel.

"Lighten the load, Dippy! She'll never clear!"

I shook my head. "Cut the fuel and I land in the Atlantic. **You don't size down at the entry — you size down at the next entry.**"

Engines roared. Wheels slogged. I felt the wires graze the undercarriage by inches and then — **green candles, baby** — we were airborne.

Behind me, Moony cackled into a shortwave radio. "He'll be in the drink by Newfoundland. Adding to short."

## Chapter 3 — The Long Drawdown

Hours bled into hours. **Fog. Ice. The engine humming like a stop order one tick from filling.** Twenty-six hours awake. My eyes refused to stay open. I held a hand outside the window and let the slipstream slap me back into the trade.

This is the part nobody talks about. Not the takeoff. Not the landing. **The middle.** When the chart goes flat and the screen goes black and every instinct screams "cut it." When you're 1,800 miles from anywhere and the only thing keeping you alive is the plan you wrote on the ground.

I held the line. **Compass east. Wings level. Don't touch the entry.**

## Chapter 4 — Le Bourget, 10:22 PM

Then — a coastline. Then a city. Then **a runway, lit by 150,000 Parisians who had stormed the airfield**. They tore my plane apart for souvenirs. They carried me on their shoulders for thirty minutes before the cops fished me out.

Thirty-three hours, thirty minutes. **One trade. One ocean. One green candle the size of an aviation revolution.**

Somewhere in Manhattan, a small grey moon stared at his broker's confirmation slip. The short had blown through margin two hours back. The covering buy filled at the top.

I cabled him from the embassy:

> *"DEAR MOONY STOP HOLDING THROUGH DRAWDOWN IS A SKILL STOP SHORTING CONVICTION IS A TUITION FEE STOP LOVE DIPPY STOP."*

He has not flown commercial since.
