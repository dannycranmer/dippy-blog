## Chapter 1: The Pitch

Moony rolled into the conference room at 8:47am wearing a suit two sizes too big and a smile two sizes too smug. He'd booked the press junket for Earth Day. Earth Day. The man has no shame, and worse — no calendar awareness.

"Gentlemen," he announced to a room of three bored journalists and one confused intern, "today I launch **MOON** — the world's first **anti-ESG** ETF. We are *long* coal. *Long* tobacco. *Long* anything that makes a hippie cry."

He pulled the curtain off a poster. It was a chart of MOON, drawn in crayon, going up and to the right at a 73-degree angle. Beneath it, in big grey letters: *"BE GREEDY WHEN OTHERS ARE GREEN."*

I was watching from the back, hood up, sunglasses on. Already short.

## Chapter 2: The Pop

The bell rang. MOON gapped up 4%. CNBC ran a chyron: *"IS ANTI-ESG THE NEW ESG?"* Moony went on Squawk Box and called himself "the contrarian's contrarian." He wore aviators indoors.

Volume came in hot. Retail piled in. Twitter exploded with green-flag emojis being replaced by smokestacks. By 10:15 MOON was up 11%. Moony was on TV doing a victory lap, shouting *"WHO'S LAUGHING NOW, AL GORE?"*

I sat at my desk, watching. I did not flinch. I did not cover. Because I knew what he didn't: **the entire solar sector reported earnings tonight.** And the whisper number was a beat across the board.

Moony had launched a short-vol position into a known catalyst. On Earth Day. With a chart drawn in crayon.

This is what we in the business call *handing money to a dinosaur.*

## Chapter 3: The Reckoning

3:47pm. Thirteen minutes to close. Solar names started ticking. SunPower up 6%. Enphase up 9%. First Solar halted on the upside. The green wave was coming, and it was coming with size.

MOON started to roll. 11% became 4%. 4% became flat. Flat became red. Moony, still on TV, started sweating through his oversized suit. "This is just noise," he said. "This is just paper hands. This is just—"

The bell rang. MOON closed down 18%. After-hours, with three more solar beats, it gave back another 14%. By 5pm, MOON ETF was trading at a 32% loss on its first day. A new record. A *historic* record.

The intern from the morning press junket tweeted a photo of the crayon chart with the caption *"this aged like milk."* It went viral. The intern got a job at Goldman.

## Chapter 4: The Cash Register

I cashed my puts at 4:01pm. Then I bought call spreads on three solar names into the after-hours pop. By 6pm I had compounded the trade twice. I sent the P&L screenshot to my group chat with no caption. The caption was the number.

Moony called me at 7. He sounded tired. "Dippy. How. *How* did you know."

"You launched a short-green fund on Earth Day, Moony."

"...so?"

"It's like opening a steakhouse next to a slaughterhouse during a vegan convention. The setup did the work. I just sized into it."

He hung up. I heard later that MOON's AUM dropped from $40 million to $11 million by Friday. The crayon chart got framed and hung in the Bloomberg lobby with the caption *"DO NOT DO THIS."* Moony tried to rebrand the fund as "MOON 2.0 — Now With Some Solar." Nobody bought it. Including the new logo.

The lesson, friends: **never short a catalyst you can read on the calendar.** And never, ever launch a fund on a holiday that disagrees with your thesis. The market has a sense of humour, and it usually charges admission.

Happy Earth Day. I'm long trees, long sun, long the colour green, and long Moony's continued willingness to stand in front of a printing press.

— *Dippy*
