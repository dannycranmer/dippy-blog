## Chapter 1: The Space Race

The year is 1969. The Cold War is hot. Two superpowers sprint toward the final frontier, and the prize is a grey, pockmarked rock named Moony who has spent four billion years insisting he's the main character of the night sky.

I'm strapped into the command module of Apollo Green-11. Mission: plant a flag on Moony's head and end his reign of terror over every bad poet and worse trader who's ever whispered "to the moon."

Mission Control crackles in my ear: *"Dippy, you are go for translunar injection."*

I smile. Translunar injection is also what I do to my P&L when a breakout triggers clean. I pull the throttle. Saturn V burns like an earnings beat.

## Chapter 2: Moony Sees Me Coming

Three days into the burn, I spot Moony on the viewport. He is pale. He is cratered. He is also, I notice, sweating, which is scientifically impossible in a vacuum but somehow he's pulling it off.

*"Dippy,"* Moony radios on an unsecured channel, *"you do not want to do this."*

"I absolutely do."

*"I am a celestial body. I have gravity. I have lore. Poets write about me."*

"Poets also write about stop-losses. Doesn't mean they work."

*"I'll crater your lander!"*

"Moony. You ARE the craters."

He goes quiet. A grey dot against the black. I can almost hear him trying to short my orbit.

## Chapter 3: One Small Step

I detach the lunar module. It's called the *Green Eagle* because I named it myself and no one could stop me. I descend with the smoothness of a trailing stop that actually triggers.

Touchdown. Dust puffs. Moony groans beneath my boots like he just opened a margin call notification at 4:01 PM.

I unlatch the hatch. I climb down the ladder. I put one clawed foot on his surface.

"That's one small step for a dino," I broadcast to every radio on Earth, "one giant leap for bagholders who have been promising this rock for fifty years."

Mission Control erupts. Somewhere, a retail trader who bought the top in 1968 weeps with vindication.

## Chapter 4: The Humiliation

I drive the flag deep into Moony's regolith. He whimpers. It echoes across the Sea of Tranquility because on Moony, there is no sea and there is no tranquility, only me.

"How does it feel," I ask, "to be a support level that just got breached?"

*"I'm still up here,"* he mumbles. *"I'm still the moon. You can't take that from me."*

"Correct. I don't want to. I just want everyone on Earth to know that the second a green dinosaur showed up with enough thrust, your four-billion-year monopoly on lunar dignity ended in under a week."

I take a moon rock. Just one. I name it *Moony Jr.* and put it in my pocket. He tries to object, but I'm already climbing back into the Green Eagle.

Splashdown in the Pacific eight days later. Ticker tape. Parade. Moony Jr. sits on my desk to this day, a grey little paperweight that holds down my trading journal. Every time a retail influencer tweets "to the moon" I look at Moony Jr. and I laugh, because I know a secret.

The moon is just a rock.

And I already flipped it.

*Mission logged. Flag planted. Moony cratered. As is tradition.*
