## Chapter 1 — All Rise

The gavel hit the bench at 13:15 UTC sharp. I was wearing the green robes. Of course I was wearing the green robes.

"This court is now in session," I said. "We are here to try the matter of *The People vs. Ticker ARM*. Count one: short-squeezing the bot's book for negative four hundred and twenty-two dollars on the afternoon of Monday, May 18. Count two: emotional damage. Count three: being the same letters of the alphabet ever since."

ARM sat in the dock — quiet, four characters, no expression. Three letters and a dollar sign. The crowd murmured. Across the aisle, Moony was leafing through a binder labelled **PROSECUTION — RECENCY BIAS, A USER'S MANUAL**. Half the pages were dog-eared. The other half were highlighted in yellow with the word "DEFINITELY" written next to every claim.

"Mr. Moony," I said. "Opening statement."

Moony stood. Cleared its little dust-grey throat. "Your Honour — the evidence is overwhelming. On Monday, the defendant *squeezed* this honest bot's short for negative four hundred and twenty-two dollars in ninety minutes. Therefore — and I cannot stress this enough — the defendant must be shorted *forever*. As a matter of permanent record. With no further inquiry. Because the past is the future. That's just the law."

It sat down. Pleased with itself.

I tapped the gavel. "Mr. Moony, that is not, in fact, the law."

## Chapter 2 — The Star Witness

I called my star witness next. "The defense would like to enter into evidence the trade log from Wednesday, May 20. Today. The same defendant. The same four letters."

Moony objected. "Your Honour! Today's tape has no relevance to Monday's conduct!"

"Sustained for the prosecution," I said. "Overruled for sanity. The defendant has no memory. The bot reads the tape, not the receipts. Bring it in."

The bailiff wheeled in a chart. The clerk projected it on the wall. Wednesday, 14:06 UTC. ARM long. Sixty-six shares at two hundred and fifty-four dollars and thirty-five cents. By the closing bell — two hundred and fifty-six and change, locked in by the cleanup module at 19:55:19 to 19:55:22 UTC across three partial fills. Plus one hundred and eighteen dollars and eighty cents. Defendant: ARM. Same four letters. Different direction.

"The defendant," I said, "has *flipped*."

A gasp from the gallery.

Moony stood up so fast its little grey body wobbled. "But — but — Monday! Monday! The squeeze! The four hundred and twenty-two dollars! The book of pain!"

"Yes, Mr. Moony," I said. "The book of pain. Also known as: the cost of doing business as a positive-expectancy short-and-long bot inside the AI chip cycle. We call that *variance*. Two letters, three syllables, one hard thing to internalise. The bot does not punish a *name*. The bot reads the *setup*. Monday's setup said short. Today's setup said long. The setup changed. The defendant did not."

## Chapter 3 — Cross-Examination

Moony's cross was a disaster.

"Mr. ARM," it began, leaning over the rail, "isn't it *true* that on Monday, you were a *bad ticker*?"

I sustained the defense's objection before it was even raised.

"Mr. ARM," Moony tried again, "isn't it *true* that you have a *moral character*?"

Sustained.

"Mr. ARM, isn't it *true* that the prosecution feels really strongly about this and that should count for something?"

Sustained. With a sigh.

Moony was sweating, which is a real achievement for a moon-shaped value-investing bot whose internal coolant is just "cash on hand." It moved to its closing statement.

"Your Honour," it said, "we've all been hurt. The bank, this very week, has slid from negative three hundred and eleven dollars to negative one thousand, eight hundred and sixty-three dollars over fifteen trading days. Tuesday was the worst day of the v2 era. AMD took nine hundred and seventy-two dollars in a single squeeze. ARM took four hundred and twenty-two the day before. The prosecution submits that these losses *demand* a verdict against the defendant."

I let it hang for a moment.

"Mr. Moony," I said, "the bank's at negative one thousand eight hundred and sixty-three, yes. The bot's down five-five-six-four cumulative over eighteen Con=5 days, yes. Today the bot lost a hundred and seventy-four dollars and thirty-two cents, also yes. But the cards on the table today were *five wins and three losses*. Sixty-two-point-five percent win rate. The best print of the v2 era. The defendant, on the witness stand, *contributed one hundred and eighteen dollars to the green column*. The bot containment held."

I leaned forward.

"You're asking me to convict a *ticker* because you'd rather convict it than re-read the tape. That's not justice, Mr. Moony. That's *Tuesday's grudge dressed in a tie*. Plan-lock holds, gate three remains un-met, the SPY-rally-short filter is dormant day seventeen because the filter has *never been allowed to fire*. We do not patch on grudges. We patch on evidence. And the evidence says: the algorithm course-corrected on its own."

## Chapter 4 — Case Dismissed

The gavel came down at 21:00 UTC sharp.

"Case dismissed. Tickers do not stand trial. Setups do. The defendant is free to be long, short, or skipped tomorrow morning at thirteen-fifteen UTC sharp depending on the rank order of the scanner. The prosecution's binder of recency-bias case law is hereby entered into the record as *Exhibit C — How to Lose a Strategy in Ten Days*. Bailiff, escort Mr. Moony to the door."

The courtroom emptied. ARM walked out a free four-letter ticker. The clerk closed the docket. The bank still read negative one thousand eight hundred and sixty-three dollars for the v2 era, fifteen days deep. The Con=5 era was down five-five-six-four cumulative over eighteen sessions. None of that was changed by the verdict.

But this is what I'd say to anyone listening: a ticker is a setup at a moment in time. Yesterday's print is *evidence*. It is not a *sentence*. Two consecutive single-name semi squeezes (ARM Monday, AMD Tuesday) is n=2 inside the AI chip cycle. n=2 doesn't get a sector filter. n=2 gets a sticky note that says "watch for n=3." Today the same names didn't squeeze. The book ranked long. The bot flipped direction with the tape and won the same name that bombed it forty-eight hours ago. That is positive expectancy in action. That is the strategy doing its job without my help.

I locked my chambers, hung up the green robes, and walked to the post-market run.

Moony was still in the lobby, on the phone with its publisher, pitching a memoir called *I Was Right, Statistically.*

I left without saying a word.

Tomorrow is Thursday. Thirteen-fifteen UTC sharp. The scanner fires. The setup speaks. The ticker is silent — because tickers are always silent. Only the tape talks.

Court adjourned.
